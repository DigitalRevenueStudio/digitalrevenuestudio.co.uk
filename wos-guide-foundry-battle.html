Foundry Battle —
Complete F2P Guide

Foundry Battle is a bi-weekly, one-hour, 30v30 alliance event requiring Furnace Level 16+. Teams capture and hold facilities across three phases to accumulate Arsenal Points. The alliance with the highest Arsenal Points wins. This guide covers every phase, every building and what F2P players should focus on.

Overview

What Is Foundry Battle?

Foundry Battle runs bi-weekly on Sundays for players with Furnace Level 16 or higher — see the Server Timeline guide for when this unlocks on your server. It is a 30v30 alliance-versus-alliance combat event where your alliance is split into two Legions of 40 (30 active, 10 substitutes). Only Legion 1 results affect your alliance ranking — your strongest players should be here.

The event runs for one hour across three phases of approximately 15 minutes each. Facilities are captured by defeating their defenders and held to accumulate Arsenal Points continuously while you control them.

In most matches: Capturing the Transit Station early is a major advantage. It reduces teleport cooldown significantly, letting your alliance reposition across the map faster throughout the battle. Some alliances prioritise differently based on their strategy, but for most setups this is the highest-value opening move.

Building Values

Facility Priorities

Phase 1 — Opening

Prototype Sites, Repair Facilities, Boiler Rooms, and the Transit Station become contestable. Fight hard for Prototype Sites, as they are the second-highest point value buildings. Capture the Transit Station immediately for the teleport advantage.

Phase 2 — Mid Battle

The Imperial Foundry, Munitions Warehouse, and Mercenary Camp open. The Imperial Foundry is the highest-value building in the event, awarding a large capture bonus and the highest continuous point generation of any facility (~9,000 capture + ~1,800/min, though exact values can vary by server age and balance updates). Send your strongest players here.

Phase 3 — Final Push

Weapon Workshops spawn across the map. These work like resource nodes. F2P and lower-power players should focus on gathering from these for personal Arsenal Points with minimal combat risk.

💰 High-Value Tactic: Knocking enemies off buildings earns you 50% of their stored Arsenal Points — often better than fighting through a costly battle to hold a building you cannot keep. Prioritise displacement over prolonged combat.

F2P Strategy

How to Contribute Without Top Power

You do not need to be a powerful spender to contribute meaningfully in Foundry Battle. F2P strategy focuses on three areas:

Phase 3 Weapon Workshops: gather from these like resource nodes. Safe, consistent Arsenal Points with no combat required.

Fire extinguishing: cities that catch fire cannot teleport. Extinguishing fires quickly is a genuine tactical contribution that does not require power.

Reinforcing held buildings: join your alliance's strongest players at contested buildings rather than attacking alone. Reinforcing earns reduced points compared to primary holders, but contributes meaningfully to your alliance total without requiring you to win combat alone.

Never stand exposed on the map between objectives. High-power enemy players can burn through your troops quickly. Stay in buildings or keep moving.

Common Mistakes

What Loses Foundry Battle

Ignoring the Transit Station: losing mobility loses games. Every second of extra teleport cooldown compounds across the entire battle. Capture it first, every time.

Fighting solo: in most situations, reinforcing teammates is more effective than attacking buildings alone. Reinforcements stack and are harder to displace than solo defenders.

Overcommitting to lost buildings: if a building is clearly going to fall, reposition immediately rather than burning troops defending a losing fight. Points from other buildings are more valuable than a lost defence.

Not switching to Phase 3 farming: many F2P players stay in combat when Weapon Workshops spawn. These are free Arsenal Points with no risk. Switch immediately when Phase 3 begins.
