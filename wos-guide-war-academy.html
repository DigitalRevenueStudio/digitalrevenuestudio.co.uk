War Academy:
Research Order and F2P Strategy

The War Academy is where you unlock higher troop tiers, improve combat stats and increase march capacity. Getting the research order right is one of the most impactful decisions in the game. This guide covers the recommended research path, tier unlock order, bottleneck nodes, F2P troop strategy and what to avoid.

Overview

How the War Academy Works

The War Academy houses your Military research tree. This is where you unlock higher troop tiers, improve troop combat stats and increase march capacity. Every node you research here permanently affects every battle, rally and event you participate in.

Research requires time, resources and in many cases prerequisite nodes to be completed first. The tree is deep and costs grow as you progress, so getting the order right from the start matters significantly for F2P players who cannot speed through every branch simultaneously.

Core rule: Always prioritise unlocking the next troop tier above combat stat improvements. A T8 troop with average stats beats a T6 troop with excellent stats. Reach the highest tier available at your Furnace level first, then return to improve stats.

Troop Tiers

The Tier Progression

Troop tiers are unlocked sequentially through War Academy research. Each new tier represents a significant power jump — the gap between tiers is much larger than the incremental gains from stat research within a tier. Furnace level gates which tiers you can unlock.

Research Path

Recommended Research Order

This is the order most F2P players follow based on community consensus. It is not the only valid path, but it avoids the most common costly mistakes.

1
Unlock next troop tier — this is always the first priority regardless of game stage. Repeat until you hit your Furnace ceiling.
2
March Capacity I — unlock early and upgrade consistently. March capacity directly affects every rally you join or lead. This is a top-3 priority for rally-active players at all stages, not just late game.
3
Primary troop type Attack I and II — once at your tier ceiling, start improving stats for your primary troop type. Infantry Attack is the most common early focus.
4
Troop Defence and Health for primary type — builds overall combat resilience. Particularly valuable for players who participate in garrison and defensive events.
5
Training Speed — speeds up how fast new troops come out of barracks. Valuable over time but not a priority until tiers and core stats are solid.
6
March Capacity II and III — expensive and long timers, but high long-term value for rally-focused play. Plan for these well in advance.
Bottleneck Nodes

The Research That Slows Everyone Down

Certain nodes in the War Academy are notorious for their cost and timer length relative to what they provide. Knowing about them in advance helps you plan rather than get blindsided.

Troop Health II and III: Among the most expensive stat nodes in the tree. High resource cost relative to the stat gain. Do not rush these — they should come after Attack and Defence improvements for your primary type.

March Capacity II: Long timers and high resource cost. Worth completing eventually but plan ahead — this is not a quick research. Save speed-ups specifically for this node if you want to push it during an event.

Troop Load nodes: Increase the resources your troops can carry when gathering. Useful for gathering builds but a low priority compared to combat and tier nodes. Many F2P players skip these entirely in the early and mid game.

Troop Training Speed: Long timers for moderate gain. Start this once core tiers and primary stat improvements are well-developed. Not worth rushing.

F2P Troop Strategy

Building Troops as F2P Player

F2P troop strategy is quality over quantity. You cannot match the raw troop numbers of heavy spenders, but you can ensure every troop you train is as strong as possible.

Always train the highest tier you have unlocked. One T9 troop is worth significantly more than three T6 troops in direct combat. The tier gap compounds dramatically at higher levels.

Upgrade existing troops before training new ones — with one important caveat. Promoting lower-tier troops to higher tiers is generally more resource-efficient than training from scratch. However, during events that score on troop training completions (Troop Training events, Power Sprint, Hero Rally), fresh training often scores better than promotions. Check the active event before deciding which approach to use.

Lower-tier troops are not always useless. Some players intentionally train lower-tier troops for Alliance Donation, Troop Promotion Events or Hospital Healing Events. Context matters. The general rule of always training the highest tier applies to combat readiness — event-specific strategies may differ.

Keep your Hospital upgraded. Hospital capacity determines how many troops survive as wounded rather than dying permanently. A full Hospital during combat means permanent troop losses. Always check before engaging in any PvP.

Auto-Join is your most important passive setting. Enable Alliance rally Auto-Join in settings. You accumulate rally rewards, hero fragments and keep your troops earning even while offline. Never leave this off.

Troop types and hero synergy: The War Academy tree has separate stat branches for Infantry, Lancers and Marksmen. Which branch to prioritise depends on your hero lineup. For a full breakdown of troop types, their strengths and weaknesses, combat roles and hero synergies, see the Troop Mastery Guide.

Alliance Research: The War Academy feeds into Alliance Technology as well. Contributing to alliance Battle tech nodes improves combat performance for your entire alliance. Check which nodes your leadership has highlighted and contribute to those first.

Common Mistakes

What Sets Back Your Research

Researching combat stat nodes before reaching your tier ceiling. This is the single most common War Academy mistake. Stat improvements at T6 are wasted the moment you unlock T8. Always push tier unlocks first.

Spreading stat research across all three troop types equally. Researching Infantry, Lancer and Marksman stats simultaneously means none of them reach meaningful levels quickly. Pick your primary troop type based on your hero lineup and go deep there first.

Ignoring March Capacity until late game. Many players treat march capacity as a late-game concern. For anyone participating in rallies — Foundry Battle, SvS, Bear Hunt — march capacity is a top-3 priority at every stage. Each additional troop in your march compounds across every event you participate in.

Using speed-ups on short research timers outside of events. The value of a speed-up is multiplied when used during an event that rewards completions. Using them casually on minor nodes between events wastes their scoring potential significantly.

Training lower-tier troops to fill queues. Keeping training queues busy by filling them with T4 or T5 troops feels productive but weakens your overall army. Idle queues are better than a growing pool of weak troops that dilute your march composition.
