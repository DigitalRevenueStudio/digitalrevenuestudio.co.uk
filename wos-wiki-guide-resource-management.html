📦 Resource Strategy

Resource Management
for F2P
Players

Every building upgrade, every troop trained, every research completed requires resources. Managing them well is not glamorous — but it is the difference between consistent progression and constantly being resource-blocked. This guide covers the habits that compound.

WOS Wiki Resource Strategy F2P April 2026
The Four Resources

What Each Resource Does

🔥 Coal
The most critical resource. Powers your Furnace — if it runs out, survivors freeze, get sick and stop working. Also used in building upgrades and troops. Never let your coal run dry. Monitor it daily.

Key habit: Do not run Furnace at Max Power constantly. Use manual controls — lower power during warm cycles, Max only during storms and night.
🌳 Wood
Primary building resource. Almost every structure and upgrade requires large amounts of wood. It is usually abundant early but becomes a bottleneck during mass upgrades.

Key habit: Keep the Sawmill upgraded and staffed. Send gathering marches to wood nodes whenever marches are free.
🍖 Meat
Feeds your survivors and troops. Troop training is a heavy meat consumer. Running out of food slows production, makes survivors unhappy and stalls training queues.

Key habit: Keep the Kitchen and Hunter's Hut fully staffed. Assign gathering heroes to Meat nodes when not needed elsewhere.
⚙ Iron
Advanced buildings and equipment. Less critical early, increasingly important for mid and late-game upgrades — especially troop and Chief Gear progression.

Key habit: Iron nodes on the world map are harder to stockpile from base production alone. Prioritise iron gathering in the mid game.
Production Habits

Making Your City Work Harder

👤
Always staff your production buildings. Coal Mine, Kitchen, Sawmill and Iron Mine need workers assigned to run at full capacity. Check your survivor assignments regularly — idle buildings are lost production you cannot recover.
📈
Upgrade production buildings whenever possible. Even one level on the Coal Mine or Sawmill increases output permanently. Prioritise these alongside Furnace upgrades rather than leaving them under-levelled.
🌡
Manage Furnace power manually. Running Max Power constantly burns through coal at an unsustainable rate. Lower power during warmer daytime cycles. Activate Max Power during blizzards and cold nights only. This single habit saves thousands of coal per day.
📦
Keep resource crates unopened. Crates in your inventory cannot be raided. Only open them when you are immediately ready to spend on an upgrade. Open resources sitting in your city are a target.
Gathering Strategy

The World Map Is Your Main Supply Chain

Base production alone is never enough beyond the early game. Gathering from resource nodes on the world map is the primary way to stockpile what you need for high-level upgrades and sustained troop training.

The overnight gather: Before you go offline, send all your marches to resource nodes. A full night of gathering while you sleep can add more resources than several hours of play. This is one of the highest-leverage habits in the game — do it every single day.

🎯
Target higher-level nodes. Level 3+ resource nodes yield significantly more per gathering trip than lower-level ones. As your troops get stronger, push to higher-level nodes — the return scales dramatically.
🛡
Gather within alliance territory. Nodes inside your alliance's territory are safer from being attacked mid-gather and often provide speed bonuses from alliance tech. Stay inside the zone whenever possible.
⚡
Use gathering boosts during Alliance Mobilisation. When the event is active and gathering is a scoring task, activate a Gathering Speed boost before sending marches. The boost stacks with hero gathering skills for a significant efficiency gain.
🧱
Prioritise Coal and Iron nodes. These are the hardest to stockpile from base production and the most likely to become upgrade bottlenecks. Wood and Meat production from buildings is relatively sufficient early — Coal and Iron need map gathering to stay ahead.

Protecting What You Have

Don't Let Raiders Take It

✓ Do

Keep resources in unopened crates in your inventory — they cannot be raided. Upgrade your Storehouse regularly to increase your protected resource cap. Use a Shield when going offline for long periods after large gathering sessions.

✕ Don't

Don't leave large quantities of open resources sitting in your city overnight without a shield. Don't gather heavily and then log off without checking your protection. Don't hoard open resources you are not immediately spending.

The storehouse rule: Resources above your Storehouse protection cap can be fully raided. Keep the Storehouse upgraded so your production surplus stays protected. And when you are about to make a big spend — upgrade, troop training, research — open the crates, spend immediately, and do not leave the surplus sitting.
