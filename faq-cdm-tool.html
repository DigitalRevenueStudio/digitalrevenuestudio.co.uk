What Is CDM 2015?

CDM stands for the Construction (Design and Management) Regulations 2015. It is the UK's principal health and safety legislation governing construction projects. CDM 2015 places legal duties on clients, principal designers, principal contractors, contractors and workers to plan, manage and monitor construction work so that risks to health and safety are properly controlled throughout the project lifecycle.

The regulations apply to virtually all construction work in the UK — from small domestic extensions to major commercial builds. Non-compliance can result in enforcement action by the HSE, project delays, and in serious cases, prosecution.

The two main phases under CDM 2015: The Pre-Construction phase covers planning, design and pre-start activities. The Construction phase covers on-site works through to practical completion. Each phase has distinct legal requirements that must be documented and evidenced.

Common Questions

Does the tool work without an internet connection?

Yes. The CDM Digital Audit Tool is fully offline-capable. Once loaded in a browser it does not require a network connection to operate. This is particularly useful on construction sites where connectivity is unreliable. Completed audits can be exported to PDF at any point.

What devices does it work on?

Any device with a modern web browser — desktop, laptop, tablet or mobile. No app download, no installation, no platform dependency. It has been tested on Chrome, Safari, Firefox and Edge. The layout is responsive and usable on a smartphone screen on-site.

How many checklist items does it cover?

45 items across the Pre-Start and Construction Phase checklists, organised into logical sections. Each item is mapped to the relevant CDM 2015 requirement and includes timing guidance — either pre-start/mobilisation or a construction phase check point.

Can I attach photo evidence?

Yes. The tool includes embedded photo evidence functionality allowing you to capture or upload images against specific checklist items. Photos are stored locally within the session and included in the PDF export, producing a single audit document containing both checklist status and supporting visual evidence.

What is the action log?

The action log is a dedicated section for recording outstanding items, remedial actions and follow-up notes that arise during an audit. It sits alongside the Pre-Start and Construction Phase checklists as a third tab in the tool. Action log entries are included in the PDF export alongside the checklist results.

Who is the tool designed for?

The tool is built for professionals with CDM 2015 duties on a construction project, including:

  • Principal Contractors conducting site audits
  • Principal Designers reviewing pre-construction compliance
  • Project Managers carrying out CDM health checks
  • Client Representatives monitoring contractor compliance
  • CDM Coordinators and SHE Advisors

It assumes familiarity with CDM 2015 and construction project structures. It is a professional audit tool, not an introductory guide to the regulations.

Does the tool replace a CDM Coordinator or SHE Advisor?

No. The tool is an audit and documentation aid — it structures and evidences the compliance checking process. It does not provide legal advice, substitute for professional CDM expertise, or replace the judgement of a qualified SHE professional. It makes the audit process faster and more consistent, not automatic.

How does the PDF export work?

At any point during or after completing an audit, you can generate a PDF report from within the tool. The export includes the project title, checklist results for both phases, any photo evidence attached, and the action log. The PDF is generated locally in the browser — no data is sent to any server.
