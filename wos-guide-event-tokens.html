Event Tokens:
When to Spend
vs When to Save

Every major event gives you tokens. The store always has more options than your tokens can cover. Get the priority order right and tokens become one of your most efficient F2P progression tools. Get it wrong and you burn them on items that barely move your account forward.

How It Works

Tokens Are a Progression Decision

Every major event gives you tokens earned through participation and completing tasks. You spend those tokens in the event reward store. The store always has more options than your tokens can cover, which means every spend is a trade-off against something else you are not buying.

Most event stores close when the event ends and unspent tokens expire. This creates pressure to spend fast. That pressure is exactly how you end up buying RSS packs when what you actually needed was hero shards. The fix is having a clear priority order before you open the store.

The core rule: Before spending anything, scroll the entire store from top to bottom. Look at everything available, then decide where your tokens do the most work. Never spend on the first item that catches your eye.

What to Spend On

The Full Priority List

Spend
Hero Shards (featured heroes)
Hero power is one of the biggest progression levers in the game. Always prioritise if a hero you are actively building is featured.
Spend
Large Construction and Research Speed-Ups
The backbone of progression. You will never have enough of these. Always take them over smaller speed-up packs.
Spend
Troop Training Speed-Ups
Troops matter significantly in mid and late game. Keep your training queues running at all times.
Spend
Gems
Almost always worth buying if available at a reasonable token cost. Gems are one of the hardest resources to come by as F2P.
Spend
Stamina and Action Point Refills
Worth buying if you are actively participating in world map events and hunts. Less valuable if your stamina is already capped.
Consider
RSS Packs (Food, Wood, Iron, Coal)
Only spend if you are actively blocked on a specific building or research right now. You can gather resources passively from the world map.
Skip
Small Speed-Ups (minutes)
Easy to farm elsewhere. Do not spend premium event tokens on 5 or 10 minute speed-ups.
Skip
Cosmetics, Frames and Decorations
Zero progression value. Never spend tokens on cosmetics while any useful item remains in the store.
When to Save

Situations Where Holding is Correct

01
Tiered Stores
If higher token totals unlock premium rewards, save until you hit that threshold rather than spending incrementally on lower-tier items first.
02
Recurring Events
Events like Bear Hunt and Hall of Chiefs repeat on a schedule. If you are close to a hero upgrade threshold, saving across two or three runs can be more efficient.
03
Hero Shard Thresholds
If you are a handful of shards away from a meaningful star level, save across multiple events to hit it in one go rather than spending tokens on secondary items.
04
Nothing Good Available
If the current store has nothing useful, check whether tokens carry over to the next cycle before deciding whether to spend or let them expire.
F2P Priority Order

The Order to Work Through

For F2P players, event tokens are one of your most reliable sources of premium items. Work through this order based on what is available in the current store:

1
Hero Shards
Always first if a hero you are actively developing is featured in the store.
2
Large Construction and Research Speed-Ups
The most universally valuable tokens in the game. Take them over everything except hero shards.
3
Troop Training Speed-Ups
Keep your training queues running. Troops compound over months.
4
Gems
Take if available at reasonable cost. One of the hardest F2P resources to accumulate.
5
Stamina Refills
Worth taking if you are actively using stamina in events and hunts.
6
RSS Packs
Only if you are genuinely blocked on an important upgrade right now. Otherwise skip.
7
Everything else
Small speed-ups and other items only if tokens remain after working through the list above.
Common Mistakes

What Most Players Get Wrong

Spending on day one before you have finished earning. You may earn more tokens before the event ends and better items may appear in the store. Always wait until you have a clear picture of your total before deciding where to spend.

Buying RSS packs instead of speed-ups. Food, wood and coal regenerate passively from gathering. Speed-ups do not. A large construction speed-up is almost always more valuable than an RSS pack of equivalent token cost.

Splitting tokens across multiple heroes. Spreading shards across three or four heroes means none of them reach the star levels where their skills become impactful. Focus on one current priority hero and funnel all hero shard tokens there.

Letting tokens expire entirely. Even if nothing in the store is ideal, construction speed-ups are almost always available and almost always useful. A suboptimal spend beats an expired one.

Not checking for daily store refreshes. Some event stores refresh daily with different items. Missing a refresh day on a priority hero can mean waiting another full cycle. Check the store each day during active events rather than once and forgetting about it.

Key Takeaways
  • Always scroll the full store before spending anything
  • Hero shards and large speed-ups come first, always
  • Check whether tokens carry over before an event ends
  • Skip cosmetics, basic speed-ups and RSS packs unless nothing better is available
  • For F2P players, event tokens are premium currency. Treat them that way.
  • Save across events if you are close to a meaningful hero upgrade threshold
  • Do not spend on day one. Wait until you have finished earning before choosing.
