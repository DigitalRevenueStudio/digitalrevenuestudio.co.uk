The Free Tool Stack We Use to
Build Digital Brands

Every brand we build starts with zero paid tools. Here is the complete stack we use across all four studio projects — tested, used daily and recommended without reservation.

6 min read Digital Revenue Studio March 2026

Every brand we build at Digital Revenue Studio starts with zero paid tools. Not because we cannot afford them — because we want to know what is genuinely achievable without a budget. If something costs money it gets disclosed. Most of what we use is free or freemium, and most of it is excellent.

Here is the complete tool stack we use across all four studio brands — tested, used daily and recommended without reservation.

Websites and hosting

GitHub Pages — free static hosting

Every brand we build launches on GitHub Pages first. Zero hosting cost, instant deployment, reliable uptime. You push a file and it is live. For static websites — which covers most of what we build — it is hard to justify paying for hosting until you have a specific reason to.

Mavericks Adventures, Digital Revenue Studio, the WOS Wiki and Atker Consultancy all launched on GitHub Pages. IONOS domain pointing is straightforward and costs only the domain registration fee.

When to upgrade: GitHub Pages works until you need server-side functionality — databases, user accounts, dynamic content. For a blog, portfolio or brand website it handles everything you need.

IONOS — domain and DNS

Low-cost domain registration and DNS management. Connecting a custom domain to GitHub Pages takes about ten minutes and a couple of DNS records. We use IONOS across all brands that have custom domains.

Email marketing

Sender — free up to 2,500 subscribers

Free tier includes up to 2,500 subscribers, 15,000 emails per month, full automation sequences and sign-up form builder. No credit card required to start. We run separate Sender accounts for DRS, Mavericks Adventures and the WOS Guide — each audience gets content relevant to them, nothing else.

The automation builder is genuinely good. Welcome sequences, conditional logic, drip campaigns — all available on the free tier. It is the email tool we recommend without qualification to anyone starting out.

Content and traffic

Reddit — primary community platform

Our highest-performing traffic source by a significant margin. One post in r/UKPersonalFinance reached 323,000 views. The WOS Guide was built from community engagement in r/whiteoutsurvival. Reddit rewards genuine contribution — posts that add real value to a community consistently outperform promotional content.

The approach: participate first, post second. Two posts per week per brand where relevant. Never promotional. Always useful.

Pinterest — evergreen traffic engine

Pinterest drives search traffic months after a pin is posted. The strategy is simple: create a pin for every blog post. Once you have ten or more posts on a brand, scale to three to five pins per post. The traffic compounds slowly but reliably. It is one of the most underused platforms for content-led brands.

Google Search Console — free indexing management

Canonical tags, sitemap submission, URL inspection, indexing requests. Essential from day one on every brand. We went from four indexed pages to eleven on the DRS site in a single day by using URL inspection correctly. Free, powerful, ignored by most people who build websites.

Publishing and products

Amazon KDP — free self-publishing

Print on demand with zero upfront costs. Global distribution across Amazon marketplaces from day one. The royalty on a children's paperback is modest but the reach is immediate. For Mavericks Adventures, KDP removed the barrier between having a book and having a book for sale everywhere.

Payhip — free digital product storefront

Five percent transaction fee on the free plan, no monthly cost. Clean product pages, instant download delivery, affiliate programme. The CDM Digital Audit Tool and WOS Lite Guide both sell through Payhip. For digital products it is our default recommendation.

Ko-fi — tip jar and support page

Zero friction to set up. Useful as a passive support option once an audience exists. We use it across WOS and DRS — not as a primary revenue source but as an available option for anyone who wants to support the work.

Analytics

Google Analytics 4 and Google Search Console on every brand property from day one. Both free, both essential. GA4 for traffic behaviour, GSC for search indexing and keyword visibility. No paid analytics tools — GA4 tells us everything we need at this stage.
