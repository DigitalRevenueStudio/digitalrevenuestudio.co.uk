Experts System:
Complete F2P Guide

The Experts system unlocks in the mid game and provides permanent passive bonuses across your city, combat and event performance. Knowing which Expert to invest in first, which to skip and how Sigils work is one of the most debated topics in WOS. This guide covers all Experts including the second generation, with priority order and F2P strategy.

Experts System ✓ Community Reviewed — April 2026

Overview

How the Experts System Works

Experts are NPC characters who provide permanent passive bonuses across your city, combat and event performance. The feature launched in August 2025 and requires Furnace Level 25 to unlock. Experts are unlocked through two routes: Tundra Trek (the F2P path, longer grind but fully accessible) and Frontier Trek (the faster pay-to-play path). Once invited to your city, Experts are upgraded at the Dawn Academy using Books of Knowledge and Expert Sigils.

There are currently three generations of Experts. Costs grow exponentially as you level up, so planning your investment order before you start spending is important.

Upfront note: Expert priorities are one of the most debated topics in WOS. The right order genuinely depends on your playstyle, alliance activities and what events you participate in. This guide presents the most common F2P consensus as a starting framework, not a fixed rule. Ask experienced players in your alliance what they prioritise and why.

Generation 1 Experts

Unlocks at ~Day 150 (Furnace 25, Fire Crystal Lv. 5)

Agnes

Agnes reduces construction time (by up to ~8 hours per upgrade at higher levels), provides daily Seeker Chests and grants extra Intel missions (roughly 1 to 3 additional Intel missions per day depending on skill level). This matters more than it first appears. Faster construction means faster Fire Crystal progression, which gates your troop tiers, Furnace levels and late-game unlocks. More Intels means more rewards and more widgets, and widgets directly scale hero combat power alongside star level. Players who underinvest in Agnes often find their progression quietly bottlenecked across multiple systems. She is considered easy to max and high priority for virtually all F2P players.

Breakpoint: Push Agnes to Casual 2 as quickly as possible. After that, steady ongoing investment. Her skill upgrades remain worthwhile throughout the game.

Cyrille

Cyrille is the Bear Huntress. She increases Bear Hunt damage significantly (community estimates suggest roughly 20 to 30% more damage output at higher skill levels, though exact values vary by server and build) and adds rally capacity for Bear hunts. Her skills for Bear Hunt rewards and Essence Stones are the primary targets. Skills 2 and 3 are the priority — these directly increase the rewards you pull from every Bear Hunt participation, and Chief Gear materials from Bear Hunt are one of the primary power levers in the mid game.

Breakpoint: Develop Cyrille alongside Agnes rather than leaving her entirely. Aim to unlock Skills 2 and 3 as your core targets. A good stopping point is Casual 1 initially, returning once Agnes is at Casual 2.

Holger

Holger focuses on Arena. He provides large Arena Shop discounts (up to ~50% off) and combat attribute bonuses for your Heroes. One active player reported gaining an extra 1 to 3 Mythic Shards daily and significantly more gear XP with Holger well-developed. Several experienced players rate him very highly because Mithril and Mythic gear are among the biggest mid-to-late game bottlenecks, and Holger is the most direct route to getting more of them. Skills 2 and 3 are the priority. Affinity Level 50 is considered a strong stopping point for F2P before costs become too high.

Romulus

Romulus provides general troop stat boosts and increases both global rally capacity and troop capacity. He also provides free daily troops at higher levels. He is the most expensive Expert to upgrade and provides the least return for casual F2P players. However, the picture changes as your server matures. In established late-game alliances, Romulus becomes genuinely valuable for anyone regularly leading SvS rallies, Fortress stacking or Foundry Battle. His rally capacity increases compound meaningfully at that level of play. For early-to-mid game F2P players, the community consensus is to leave him until all other Experts are well-developed.

F2P note: Skip Romulus or deprioritise heavily in the early and mid game. He becomes more relevant later, particularly for R4/R5 players and regular rally leaders in competitive alliances.

Generation 2 Experts

Unlocks at ~Day 195 (Hero Generation 4)

Baldur

Baldur deserves special emphasis. He is not just another Expert — he is a multiplier for all the others. His primary skill generates Expert Sigils, which are the upgrade currency for every other Expert in the game. Without Baldur active, F2P progression through the Expert system slows dramatically. With Baldur's Sigil skill running, all your other Experts accelerate. For many F2P players, Baldur will be their only consistent source of Sigils — roughly ~15 free Sigils per week at a solid development level (exact amount varies by skill level). To put that in context, most Expert skills cost between 10 and 30 Sigils, so Baldur alone can cover 1 to 2 skill unlocks per week across your other Experts.

He also boosts points and rewards in alliance events including Alliance Championship and Crazy Joe, providing additional value beyond the Sigil engine.

Baldur is a multiplier, not just an expert. Every Sigil he generates funds Agnes, Cyrille, Holger and every other Expert you develop. Getting his Sigil skill active early is one of the highest-leverage moves available to F2P players. Save Books of Knowledge and Sigils ahead of his unlock so you can invest immediately on arrival.

Breakpoint: Rush Skill 2 (Sigil generation) immediately. This is the priority above everything else on Baldur. Once Skill 2 is active, continue steady investment.

Fabian

Fabian earns additional tokens from the Foundry Battle shop and has a skill that instantly heals a set number of troops during Foundry Battle (players report healing significant troop numbers without leaving the event for the usual ~12 minute hospital wait). His other skills are mainly useful for rally leaders. If you participate regularly in Foundry Battle, he has meaningful value. If you do not, he can be deprioritised significantly. Level 60 gets most of his useful skills to a high level and is a reasonable stopping point before costs become excessive.

Valeria

Valeria boosts SvS points, provides State of Power Tokens (used to buy Fire Crystals and Shards — typically worth several hundred tokens per SvS cycle depending on skill level) and increases SvS rally capacity. Her second skill occasionally offers Expert Sigils in her shop, which should always be bought when available. Her capacity and stat boost skills are generally considered low value for F2P. She is worth investing in once Gen 1 Experts are well-developed. Save Sigils and Books of Knowledge ahead of her unlock.

Generation 3 Experts

Newest Additions (2026)

Ronne

Ronne is a legendary mercenary added in March 2026. She specialises in the Tundra Trade Route event, providing attack and defence bonuses for truck raiding, enhanced cargo gathering and additional daily truck refreshes. Based on early community signals, the consensus is fairly clear: she is a niche, event-specific Expert with limited utility outside of a monthly event. She is good for squeezing more resources from the Trade Route as F2P, but her design clearly positions her as a late priority. Invest in her only after Gen 1 and Gen 2 Experts are well-developed.

Kathy

Kathy is the Mine Leader, added in April 2026 with a focus on the Frostfire Mine event. She is brand new and the community is still forming a view on her priority. However, her design angle is worth paying attention to. Frostfire Mine is a bi-weekly event and one of the primary sources of Orichalcum, which is used to craft elite Chief Gear. Unlike Ronne's monthly event, Frostfire Mine runs every two weeks, which means Kathy's value compounds more frequently. Early signals suggest she may have meaningfully higher practical value than Ronne for active players who prioritise gear progression. Watch how the community evaluates her over the next few weeks before committing significant resources.

Watch this space on Gen 3. Ronne looks like a clear niche pick. Kathy's connection to Orichalcum and bi-weekly Frostfire Mine gives her a stronger case for practical F2P value, but the community needs more time with both before definitive priority guidance is possible. Check in with your alliance and current Reddit discussion before investing heavily in either.

Breakpoints

When to Stop and Save

Expert upgrade costs grow exponentially. Knowing where to pause investment and redirect resources is as important as knowing where to start. These are the key stopping points the community has identified:

Agnes: Push to Casual 2 as fast as possible. After that, steady ongoing investment is worthwhile throughout the game.

Cyrille: Unlock Skills 2 and 3 as your core targets. Casual 1 initially, return after Agnes reaches Casual 2.

Holger: Pause at Affinity Level 50. Skills 2 and 3 first. The return on investment up to this point is extremely high. Beyond Level 50 costs escalate sharply relative to benefit.

Baldur: Rush Skill 2 (Sigil generation) immediately on unlock. This is the single most important action. Then continue steady development.

Fabian: Level 60 gets most of his useful skills to a solid level and is a reasonable stopping point. Beyond this costs climb steeply.

F2P Strategy

Getting the Most from Limited Resources

Expert upgrade costs grow exponentially. Going deep on your priority Experts rather than spreading resources thin is more efficient than trying to level everyone evenly. Once Baldur is available, getting his Sigil skill active quickly matters because those Sigils fund upgrades across all your other Experts. To put the compounding effect in perspective: ~15 Sigils per week over 6 months is roughly 390 Sigils, enough to unlock several skills across multiple Experts entirely from Baldur alone.

Save Sigils and Books of Knowledge ahead of Gen 2 Expert unlocks (Baldur and Valeria) so you can invest in them quickly on arrival. The same logic applies to Ronne and Kathy as Gen 3 Experts, though the community is still establishing priority for those two.

Community note: Expert priority is one of the most debated topics in WOS and the right answer depends on your playstyle and goals. The order above reflects common F2P consensus but your alliance's R4/R5 will have views based on your specific server meta. Ask them before committing significant Books of Knowledge to any Expert.

Common Mistakes

What Slows Your Expert Progress

Spreading Books of Knowledge across every Expert evenly. This is the single most common mistake. Upgrading every Expert a little at a time means none of them reach the skill breakpoints that deliver real value. Go deep on 1 to 2 Experts first rather than spreading thin across all of them.

Ignoring Baldur or delaying him. Players who do not prioritise Baldur's Sigil skill often find themselves starved of upgrade currency months later. Those ~15 Sigils per week compound dramatically over time. Every week you delay Baldur's Skill 2 is a week of free Sigils you cannot recover.

Investing heavily in Romulus too early. Romulus costs significantly more than other Experts at equivalent levels and the return for F2P players in the early and mid game is low. Money spent on Romulus before Agnes, Cyrille, Holger and Baldur are well-developed is almost always a suboptimal use of resources.

Not saving ahead of Gen 2 unlocks. Baldur and Valeria unlock around Day 195 and both benefit from immediate investment. Players who have not saved Sigils and Books of Knowledge ahead of this point often have to wait weeks before they can activate the skills that matter most.

Skipping Holger because Arena feels optional. Arena provides Mithril and Mythic gear through its shop. At higher skill levels Holger provides discounts of roughly up to ~50% in that shop. Players who deprioritise Holger often find gear progression becoming their primary bottleneck in the mid-to-late game, typically around Day 200 onwards.

Spending Books of Knowledge outside of SvS without a plan. Some players spend as soon as they accumulate resources. Others save specifically for SvS scoring windows. Both approaches have merit, but spending blindly without knowing which approach suits your alliance is a common source of regret.
