The Labyrinth
Complete F2P
Strategy Guide

The Labyrinth unlocks at Furnace Level 19 and runs on a weekly reset cycle. Most players underperform here because they use the wrong troop ratios for each zone. This guide covers everything — zone schedule, troop ratios, squad strategy and shop priorities.

The Basics

How the Labyrinth Works

🔓

How to Unlock

Requires Furnace Level 19 and a Level 1 Command Center. On new servers it typically opens around week 2. Some zones have additional unlock requirements.

⚔

Combat System

Uses Expedition stats and skills — not Exploration stats. Think PvE combat like Beast or Mercenary fights, not Arena. This distinction changes everything about which heroes you use.

🔄

Weekly Reset

Stage progress resets every Monday when the underground world fills with mist. After Monday reset you can re-challenge cleared stages for Raid Rewards. The Raid (instant clear) feature unlocks after clearing Stage 10.

⏳

Daily Attempts

You have 5 challenge attempts per zone daily, refreshing at 00:00 UTC. Use all 5 — don't be afraid to fail. Trying different troop ratios and hero combinations is how you find what works for your account.

🧱

Troops Provided

Monday–Saturday zones use Level 10 troops provided by the Labyrinth — no troop loss risk. Sunday's Gaia Heart zone uses your own troops, but you still won't suffer casualties or wounded troops.

🏆

Best Rewards

Prioritise exchanging Labyrinth tokens for Mithril and Fire Crystal Shards. These are the highest long-term value items in the shop and are hard to source elsewhere as F2P.

Zone Schedule

Which Zone Opens Each Day

Mon & Tue
Land of the Brave — Stat source: Heroes, Hero Gear, Exclusive Gear. Focus on hero expedition skills and gear quality.
Wed & Thu
Cave of Monsters / Glowstone Mine — Stat source: Pets and Pet Skills (Cave), Chief Charms (Glowstone). Glowstone requires Furnace 25.
Fri & Sat
Earthlab / Dark Forge — Stat source: Research Center and War Academy tech. Dark Forge requires Furnace 22 with Chief Gear.
Sunday
Gaia Heart — Uses your own troops (no loss risk). Requires 3 squads. Higher tier Fire Crystal and Helios troops have a real advantage here.

Key principle: Adjust your gear and hero setup based on which zone is open that day. The stat source changes daily — the same formation will not perform equally across all zones. F2P players should manually rotate gear between squads to maximise performance with limited resources.

Troop Ratios

The Right Ratio for Each Zone

The AI enemy in most zones defaults to a 33/33/33 split (Infantry/Lancer/Marksman), except Stage 10 which shifts to roughly 53/27/20. Adjusting your ratio to counter the enemy composition directly impacts your clear rate.

Zone Squads Recommended Ratio Alternative Notes
Land of the Brave 1 or 3 50/20/30 52/13/35 3-squad zones add RNG from turn order — heavier Infantry handles multi-round losses better
Cave of Monsters 1 45/10/45 55/15/30 Balanced Infantry-Marksman works well here. Adjust based on your battle reports
Glowstone Mine 1 50/15/35 45/20/35 Requires Furnace 25 + Chief Charm
Earthlab 1 60/15/25 50/20/30 Heavy Infantry recommended — check battle report and adjust if Infantry line collapses
Dark Forge 1 45/20/35 40/20/40 Requires Furnace 22 + Chief Gear. Balanced approach works here
Gaia Heart 3 60/40/0 54/3/43 Your own troops used. See 3-squad strategy below

Read your battle reports. If your Infantry line is dying before the fight ends, increase Infantry. If you are winning easily, shift more to Marksman for damage. These ratios are starting points — your hero stats and gear will mean the optimal ratio is slightly different for every account.

Gaia Heart — 3 Squad Strategy

The F2P Three Squad Approach

Land of the Brave and Gaia Heart require 3 squads. The F2P approach is to rotate your single set of top-tier gear across squads to create two effective formations rather than three weak ones.

Squad 1 — Main
60/40/0

Full Infantry + Lancer. Best heroes wearing all your gear. Greg, Lynn, Bradley are ideal — Greg reduces enemy damage and boosts HP. Your primary damage force.

Squad 2 — Support
54/3/43

Mia in lead — she buffs all troop types so minimal Lancer is fine. Gear rotated from Squad 1 after its fight. The 3% Lancer is solely to trigger Lancer hero skills.

Squad 3 — Reserve
50/20/30

Standard ratio with remaining heroes. Marksman heroes here can strip gear — they're deployed for their expedition skill global buffs, not their damage.

Shop Priorities

What to Spend Your Labyrinth Tokens On

1
Mithril
Primary shop priority. Essential for crafting elite Chief Gear and hard to obtain in meaningful quantities elsewhere as F2P.
2
Fire Crystal Shards
T11 troop upgrade material. Buy every time it appears — long-term progression value is enormous.
3
Chieftain Accessories
After Mithril and Fire Crystal Shards are covered, Chieftain Accessories are a solid third priority.
4
Chief Gear Materials
Equipment materials for upgrading Chief Gear. Worth picking up once higher priorities are purchased.
5
Hero Shards
Only if a current-generation hero you are actively building is featured. Skip if your hero roster is solid.
✕
Resources & Basic Speedups
Avoid unless you have exhausted all better options. Poor token value relative to everything above.
