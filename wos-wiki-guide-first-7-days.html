Your first week in Whiteout Survival sets the foundation for everything that follows. Most new players get it wrong not because they play badly, but because nobody tells them what actually matters first. This guide does.

The One Rule

The Furnace Is Everything

Every decision in your first week should be filtered through a single question: does this help me upgrade my Furnace? The Furnace determines the temperature zone of your city, unlocks buildings, and — critically — determines what tier of troops you can train. A Furnace 26 player with T9 troops will dismantle a Furnace 22 player with T7 troops regardless of how well they play everything else.

The golden rule: Always check what your next Furnace level requires. Then focus 100% of your resources and speedups on those specific buildings. Ignore everything else until the Furnace is done.

Building Priority

What to Build First

Not all buildings are equal. In your first week, only upgrade buildings that are required for your next Furnace level, plus these core survival buildings:

1
Furnace
Your top priority at all times. Every other building exists to support Furnace upgrades. Always be working towards the next Furnace level.
2
Coal Mine & Kitchen
Coal keeps your Furnace running. The Kitchen feeds your survivors. Always keep both fully staffed with survivors — these two buildings are non-negotiable.
3
Shelter & Clinic
Keeps survivors warm and healthy. Sick or unhappy survivors work slower, reducing your resource production. Upgrade both steadily alongside the Furnace.
4
Lumber Yard & Iron Mine
Wood and Iron are required for most Furnace upgrades. Keep them progressing so you are never resource-blocked when the Furnace is ready.
5
Research Center
Always keep research active. Economy and survival research before combat. Faster production and lower consumption help you forever — start it early and never let it sit idle.
✕
Everything Else
Upgrade only when required for Furnace progression. Do not spread resources thin by upgrading decorative or non-critical buildings in week one.
Day by Day

Your First 7 Days

1
Survive & Set Up
Complete the tutorial, get your foundations in place
  • Complete all Chapter Missions — they guide you through the tutorial and give excellent rewards
  • Staff your Coal Mine and Kitchen immediately — never leave these idle
  • Upgrade the Furnace to level 3 or 4 using tutorial speedups
  • Unlock Exploration and send your first hero squad — do this early, it runs passively
  • Activate your beginner shield — it protects you while you are still setting up
  • Redeem gift codes now — free resources from day one
2
Join an Alliance
One of the highest-leverage moves in the game
  • Join an active alliance today — not just any alliance. Look for one in the top 5 of your state that is actively communicating
  • Teleport near your alliance city immediately after joining — solo players are easy targets
  • Alliance membership unlocks Bear Hunt, Crazy Joe, and shared building speed boosts
  • Ask your R4/R5 leaders what the alliance rules are — some alliances have zeroing rules, NAP agreements and rally coordination
  • Keep pushing Furnace upgrades — check daily mission progress for extra Gems
3
Hero Focus Begins
Decide who you are building — and only build them
  • Do not upgrade heroes equally — pick 2–3 and focus all XP, shards and skills on those
  • Best early F2P heroes to build first:
  • Molly — Lancer, versatile, easy to star up. Build her first.
  • Sergey — Infantry, strong defensive hero. Excellent for Crazy Joe.
  • Natalia — Infantry attacker. Note: only available via VIP packs — not accessible to true F2P players. If you have her, build her. If not, focus on Molly and Sergey.
  • Prioritise Molly's Exploration Skill 1 and Expedition Skill 2 early — she pays back every resource invested
  • Do not waste Skill Manuals on blue (Epic) combat heroes — save those for purple and gold heroes. Exception: blue gathering heroes. Their gathering bonus skills are worth investing in — they improve your daily resource income every single day.
  • Keep Exploration running constantly — it generates Hero XP passively
4
Speedup Discipline
The habit that separates strong F2P players from weak ones
  • Stop burning small speedups on short timers. Save them for long Furnace upgrades and events
  • The only valid reason to use a speedup in week one: a Furnace upgrade that would otherwise sit overnight or through an important event
  • Gems follow the same rule — do not spend Gems casually. Save for Lucky Wheel during Hall of Chiefs
  • Start the habit now: every resource you save this week compounds into a lead over players who burned everything early
  • Keep research running — Economy branch first
5
Troops & Training
Always be training
  • Always have troops training. Empty training queues are wasted time
  • Troop priority: Infantry first (defensive frontline), Marksman second (main damage), Lancers last
  • Upgrade lower-tier troops to higher tiers before training new ones — upgrading is more resource-efficient than training fresh
  • Unlock Bear Hunt if available — it is one of the best sources of Essence Stones for Chief Gear later
  • Do your 5 daily Arena battles — they take minutes and give you Arena tokens for gear
6
Gather & Protect
Resources are the lifeblood of progression
  • Send gathering marches whenever they are free — never leave them idle
  • Use your shield if going offline for long periods — especially after gathering large amounts
  • Keep your city inside the alliance zone — players far from their alliance are easier targets
  • Check daily missions and complete as many as possible — the 7-day login rewards are significant
  • Chapter Missions give some of the best early-game rewards — work through them consistently
7
Review & Set Direction
Assess where you are and what comes next
  • Check your Furnace level — where do you need to be by Day 70? Target Furnace 30 before the Fire Crystal era begins
  • Are your heroes progressing? Pick your main 2–3 and make sure all resources are going to them
  • Is your alliance active? If not, consider switching now while it is early
  • Claim your 7-day login reward chest — it is one of the best beginner rewards in the game
  • Look ahead to Hall of Chiefs — it comes early and rewards players who have been saving speedups and resources
Common Mistakes

What to Avoid

Upgrading everything evenly
Spreading resources across every building slows your Furnace. Focus only on what is required for the next Furnace level and your core survival buildings.
Burning speedups on short timers
5-minute speedups used on 10-minute timers are a habit that compounds into weeks of lost progression. Save them for events and long upgrades.
Upgrading every hero equally
Splitting shards, XP and skill manuals across every hero leaves you with a roster of weak heroes instead of 2–3 strong ones. Pick your core team and commit.
Staying isolated or in a weak alliance
A solo player or player in an inactive alliance gets hit, loses resources, and falls behind fast. Join an active top-5 alliance and stay in their zone.
Ignoring Exploration
Exploration generates Hero XP and resources passively. Set it going and keep it running — not doing this is leaving free progression on the table every day.
Spending Gems casually
Gems are your most valuable F2P currency. Save them for Lucky Wheel spins during Hall of Chiefs to unlock key heroes. Never spend them on speedups.

The mindset that matters most: In WOS, patience is a strategy. Every speedup you save, every Gem you don't spend, every hero shard you hold — they compound. The players who look unbeatable at Day 90 are almost always the ones who were the most disciplined in their first 7 days.
