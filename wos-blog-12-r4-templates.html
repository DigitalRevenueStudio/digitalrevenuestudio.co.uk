Alliance Tools

Copy-Paste Alliance Templates.
Are They Actually Useful?

New to the R4 or R5 role and not sure what your alliance needs to know before SvS or Flame and Fangs? We have you covered. And if you are an experienced leader who just does not have templates yet, these work for you too.

Digit • Digital Revenue Studio April 2026

WOS does let you create saved templates in game. If you are an experienced R4 or R5 you may already have your own. But if you are new to the role, or you have just been promoted and you are not sure what your alliance actually needs to know before SvS or Flame and Fangs, that is exactly the gap these templates are built for.

New R4s and R5s often know the game well but have not had to coordinate an entire alliance around an event before. What do you send? When do you send it? What does it need to say? These templates answer all three questions.

And if you are an experienced leader who just does not have templates yet — or yours need updating — they are just as useful for you.

What the Templates Are

The R4/R5 Resources page contains pre-formatted alliance chat templates that copy and paste directly into WOS. The formatting is correct for in-game chat — no reformatting needed, no manual spacing. Tap copy, paste into chat, send.

The current templates cover three scenarios. The SvS prep week schedule shows the full five-day task breakdown in a column format your whole alliance can read at a glance. The matchmaking coordination template covers the Saturday and Sunday UTC power reduction window with the key actions clearly listed. The Flame and Fangs schedule shows the Sunday hold mechanic and the Mon and Tue session times.

The formatting took a lot of testing. WOS alliance chat handles Unicode differently to most text editors. The templates use specific fullwidth characters and emoji anchors on every line so they render correctly regardless of device.

Why This Does Not Exist Elsewhere

There are WOS calculators, tier lists and event guides across dozens of sites. We have not seen pre-formatted copy-paste chat templates anywhere. The closest things are Reddit threads with suggested wording that inevitably needs reformatting before it works in game.

The R4/R5 resources page is designed to be the tool we wished existed when coordinating alliances. More templates are coming including Bear Hunt rally coordination and Alliance Championship lane assignments.

Are They Actually Useful?

The SvS prep template is based directly on the format used by Slick78 from [TJW], State 3217, which we know works because we have seen it in action. The matchmaking template is built on community consensus tactics that experienced players use every SvS cycle.

We would rather be honest: the templates work in game but whether your alliance uses them effectively depends on your R4 and R5s putting them out at the right time. The tool is only as good as the coordination behind it.
