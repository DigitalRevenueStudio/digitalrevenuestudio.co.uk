🤝 Alliance Guide

Alliance Tips
Joining, Contributing
& Progressing

Your alliance is not just a clan tag. It determines your protection, your event rewards, your resource gathering speed, and how fast your city grows. Choosing the right one — and contributing correctly — is one of the highest-leverage decisions you make in WOS.

Why It Matters

What Your Alliance Actually Does For You

New players often treat alliances as background — a box to tick. The reality is that an active, well-organised alliance accelerates your progression in ways that cannot be replicated solo. Passive production boosts, shared events, protection from attacks, and access to Alliance technology all compound over time.

🛡
Protection
Most states operate NAP agreements (non-aggression pacts). Being in a top-5 alliance means attackers think twice. Solo players and weak-alliance members are easy targets for resource raids.
⚡
Alliance Technology
Alliance tech boosts gathering speed, construction speed and troop stats for every member. A well-developed alliance tech tree gives significant passive advantages — you benefit just by being a member.
🎯
Events
Bear Hunt, Crazy Joe, Alliance Mobilisation, Alliance Championship — the best events in the game are alliance-only. You simply cannot access them without an active alliance.
📍
Territory & Gathering
Alliance territory provides faster and safer resource gathering nodes. Gathering inside your alliance's territory reduces risk and often gives a speed bonus. Position your city within the alliance zone.
Finding the Right One

How to Choose Your Alliance

Not all alliances are equal. Joining the wrong one is almost as bad as being solo. Here is what to look for:

✅
Join a top-5 alliance in your state. Look at the Alliance Rankings — top-5 alliances have the most territory, the strongest tech, and the best event participation. The difference in progression between a top-5 and a bottom-10 alliance is significant.
✅
Check activity before committing. Look at the alliance chat before joining. Is anyone talking? Are events being organised? A silent alliance is usually an inactive one. Active chat and regular event announcements are the signs you want.
✅
Teleport to the alliance zone immediately after joining. Do not sit outside the alliance territory. A city isolated from its alliance is a target. Move within the zone the day you join.
✅
Read the alliance rules. Most organised alliances have rules — NAP agreements with other alliances, rules about attacking, rally obligations, event participation expectations. Know them before you act.
✅
Do not stay in an inactive alliance out of loyalty. If your alliance stops running events, stops communicating, or starts shrinking — move. Your progression suffers directly. Switch early while your options are still open.

Unlock requirement: You need Furnace Level 7 to access the Alliance tab. Do not wait — join as soon as it unlocks. Every day without an alliance is a day of missed tech bonuses, missed event points and missed protection.

How to Contribute

What F2P Players Should Actually Do

You do not need to be a big spender to be a valuable alliance member. Consistent contribution in the right areas makes more difference than occasional big spending.

💪
Donate to Alliance Technology daily. Every donation contributes to tech that benefits everyone. Prioritise tech that boosts gathering speed and construction — these compound across all members over time.
💪
Be online for events. Crazy Joe, Bear Hunt and Alliance Championship all reward online participation heavily. Waves 7, 14 and 17 of Crazy Joe only target active players — being online during these is directly worth points for everyone.
💪
Fill Bear Hunt rallies. Even if you cannot lead a rally, joining with all your stamina contributes directly to the alliance score. Empty stamina on the bear — every time.
💪
Help with construction. When an alliance member posts a construction request, tap the help button. It costs you nothing and saves them real time. This is one of the easiest ways to be a good alliance member.
💪
Communicate. If you are going offline for an extended period, say so. If an important event is happening and you cannot attend, let your R4/R5 know. Silence is mistaken for inactivity and leads to being removed from good alliances.
Alliance Ranks

What the Ranks Mean

RankRoleWhat They Do
R5LeaderFull control — sets rules, manages membership, calls major events, controls alliance territory decisions
R4OfficerCan start events (Bear Hunt, Crazy Joe), manage members, issue instructions. The people who run day-to-day operations
R3EliteTrusted members — often experienced players given more responsibility. May assist with rally coordination
R2VeteranActive members who have proven themselves over time
R1MemberStandard starting rank for new joiners. Follow instructions, participate in events, donate to tech

As a new F2P member: You will start at R1. Focus on consistent participation, filling rallies and donating to tech. Rank promotions come from demonstrating reliability — not power. Small accounts who show up for every event are valued more than large accounts who are never online.

Alliance Events

The Events That Matter Most

Bear Hunt
Runs every ~47 hours. R4/R5 opens a rally against a giant bear. Send all stamina every time — rewards include Essence Stones for Chief Gear. Join the strongest rally available, not your own.
Crazy Joe
20 waves of bandits, 40 minutes. Send all Infantry and Lancers to allies before it starts. Be online for waves 7, 14 and 17 — elite waves that only target active players for double points.
Alliance Mobilisation
Multi-day event with daily tasks — gathering, hunting, construction. Coordinate with your alliance on which tasks to focus. Completing alongside other members multiplies rewards.
Alliance Championship (Fortress Battle)
Two alliances fight for control of buildings. Teleport management is critical — do not waste teleports chasing kills. Capture the Arsenal and Foundry for points. Weaker players should reinforce buildings held by strong teammates.
Polar Terror Rallies
Alliance members call rallies against Polar Terrors on the map. Enable auto-join so you automatically participate even while offline. Highest stamina-to-points ratio of any event — especially during SvS Beast Slay day.
