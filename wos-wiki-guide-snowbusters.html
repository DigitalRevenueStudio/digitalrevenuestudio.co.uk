Snowbusters
Complete Event
Guide

Snowbusters looks simple — drive a snowplow, clear snow. The depth is in resource management. Get the gear upgrade order right, move in one consistent direction, and you will clear stages efficiently and earn significantly better rewards than players who just roam the map randomly.

WOS Guide Snowbusters F2P Tips March 2026
The Three Resources

Fuel, Health and Coal

⛽
Fuel
Consumed by movement, snow clearing and fighting. Starts at 200. Regenerates at 12 every 5 minutes. Runs out = stop. Never waste it on the last 5% of snow — use the rocket at 95%.
❤
Health
Drops when fighting enemies or taking damage. Zero health means you drop all your Coal. Manage health via the central fire and medkits. Stay close to fire when possible.
💨
Coal
Used to upgrade the Fire Pit each stage. Leftover coal converts to bonus fuel at stage end. Only upgrade the Fire Pit when necessary — save the rest for conversion.
Gear Upgrade Order

Upgrade in This Exact Order

Gear resets after every Snowbusters event so there is no carrying upgrades forward. The goal is to invest your Toolkits in the order that gives the most benefit during the current event. This is the F2P priority order:

1
Gloves
Boosts health and reduces fuel consumption. Directly extends how long you can keep clearing snow — the most impactful early upgrade for F2P players.
2
Coat
Improves damage resistance and — critically — determines how fast your Fuel recovers. A higher Coat level gives you more total Fuel across the entire event. Upgrade this on Day 1 if possible for maximum effect.
3
Snowbuster
Increases attack power and snow-clearing range. Useful but lower priority than the survival-focused upgrades above. Upgrade once Gloves and Coat are handled.
4
Boots
Increases movement speed and improves fire regeneration. Genuinely useful but the last priority — the other three gear pieces affect your core loop more directly.

Coat is the key to Fuel recovery. The level of your Coat directly determines how fast your Fuel regenerates and how much total Fuel you get during the whole event. Upgrading Coat to maximum level on Day 1 pays dividends across all three days — every subsequent run you play will have more fuel available than if you upgraded it later.

Clearing Strategy

How to Clear Efficiently

➡
Always clear in one direction in a line. Moving systematically in one direction is the single most fuel-efficient clearing method. Zigzagging and backtracking wastes fuel on movement without clearing new snow.
🧍
Rescue survivors first. Each survivor you rescue increases your snow-clearing radius by 10%. Survivors near the Fire Pit should always be your first priority when entering a new stage — they make every subsequent action more efficient.
🎯
Stop at 95% — use the rocket. Once you hit 95% snow cleared, the rocket activates automatically. Use it immediately rather than manually clearing the remaining 5%. Anything beyond 95% is pure wasted fuel.
🔥
Upgrade the Fire Pit early — but only when necessary. A higher Fire Pit level increases HP regeneration rate, reducing reliance on medkits. Upgrade it early in each stage but stop once you have enough regen to sustain fights. Save remaining Coal for end-of-stage fuel conversion.
💥
Stack boosts — Super Snowbuster + Infini-Fuel together. When both are active simultaneously, only clear snow during that window. This combination is the highest-efficiency clearing state in the game. Do not waste either boost on areas you were going to clear anyway.
💰
Bonus stages (5, 10, 15) give unlimited Fuel. These are your opportunity to clear everything on the map — all chests, survivors and snow. Do not finish a bonus stage early just because there are no enemies left. Exhaust it completely before moving on.
Snowmen and Beasts

How to Handle Enemies

❅ Snowmen — Avoid Unless Needed
  • Snowmen drop Coal and Fuel when defeated — but the fuel cost of fighting them usually outweighs the reward
  • Do not chase snowmen across the map. Clear snow in your lane first
  • Only engage if one is directly in your clearing path or you have surplus fuel
🦌 Elk / Animals — Avoid
  • Elk and animals are obstacles — they will not help you clear stages faster
  • Navigate around them rather than engaging
  • Walking near the Fire Pit resets monster aggression if you are being chased
🐻 Beasts — Prioritise in Beast Stages
  • Beasts drop Map Fragments tied to the event theme — essential for unlocking cosmetic rewards
  • In stages that have a beast, clear only enough snow to make a walking path to the beast first
  • Lure beasts near medkits before engaging so you can heal mid-fight
  • Use Portable Snowshield to survive longer during fights
🚀 Rockets — Save Them
  • V2 Rockets are rare and carry over to the next Snowbusters event — never use them early
  • Only use rockets to destroy dark and thick ice blocks after Stage 20
  • Stack rockets across multiple events then use them all in one session to push deeper stages
  • Standard rockets from stage rewards do not carry over — use those freely

Fuel Supply Chests carry over — Coal does not. Do not open Fuel Supply Chests unless you can use the fuel immediately. Opened fuel that you do not use is lost when the event ends. Unopened chests stay in your backpack for the next event.
