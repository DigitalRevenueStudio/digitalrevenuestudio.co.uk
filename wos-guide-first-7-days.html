The One Rule

The Furnace Is Everything

Every decision in your first week should be filtered through a single question: does this help me upgrade my Furnace? The Furnace determines the temperature zone of your city, unlocks buildings and determines what tier of troops you can train.

A Furnace 26 player with T9 troops will dismantle a Furnace 22 player with T7 troops regardless of how well the lower-Furnace player manages everything else. Furnace level is the single most important progression metric in the entire early game.

The golden rule: Always check what your next Furnace level requires. Then focus 100% of your resources and speed-ups on those specific buildings. Ignore everything else until the Furnace is done.

Building Priority

What to Build First

Not all buildings are equal. In your first week, only upgrade buildings that are required for your next Furnace level, plus these core survival buildings:

1
Furnace
Your top priority at all times. Every other building exists to support Furnace upgrades. Always be working towards the next Furnace level.
2
Coal Mine and Kitchen
Coal keeps your Furnace running. The Kitchen feeds your survivors. Always keep both fully staffed with survivors. These two buildings are non-negotiable.
3
Shelter and Clinic
Keeps survivors warm and healthy. Sick or unhappy survivors work slower, reducing resource production. Upgrade both steadily alongside the Furnace.
4
Lumber Yard and Iron Mine
Wood and Iron are required for most Furnace upgrades. Keep them progressing so you are never resource-blocked when the Furnace is ready.
5
Research Center
Always keep research active. Economy and survival research before combat. Faster production and lower consumption help you forever. Start it early and never let it sit idle.
✕
Everything Else
Upgrade only when required for Furnace progression. Do not spread resources thin by upgrading decorative or non-critical buildings in week one.
Day by Day

Your First 7 Days

Days 1-2
Furnace rush and alliance
Complete all Chapter Missions as they appear. They guide you through the tutorial and give excellent early rewards. Join an alliance as soon as Furnace Level 7 unlocks.
  • Complete Chapter Missions for rewards
  • Staff Coal Mine and Kitchen immediately
  • Join an active alliance at Furnace Level 7
  • Unlock Exploration and send your first hero squad
Days 3-4
Research and gathering
Start queuing research. Economy and survival nodes first. Send gathering marches to world map nodes whenever all march slots are free. Coal management becomes critical here.
  • Start your first research queue and never let it idle
  • Send all free marches to resource nodes on the world map
  • Monitor coal production and lower Furnace power when offline
  • Start participating in Bear Hunt if your alliance has it active
Days 5-7
Troops and progression
Begin training troops once the Barracks is available. Train the highest tier available to you. Start accumulating speed-ups for future Hall of Chiefs events rather than spending them casually.
  • Start training troops and keep the queue running
  • Upgrade Sergey and Bahiti (your starting heroes) to be functional
  • Save speed-ups rather than burning them on short timers
  • Teleport your city inside your alliance territory if you have not already

The alliance shortcut: Joining a good alliance on Day 1 and asking an active member who is 2 to 4 weeks ahead of you for specific advice will compress your first week significantly. Ask targeted questions ("I am about to upgrade X, should I do Y first?") rather than general ones. Players slightly ahead have just solved the exact problems you are currently facing.

Common Mistakes

What to Avoid

Upgrading buildings other than the Furnace first. Every resource and speed-up spent on a non-Furnace building in week one is a direct delay to your most important progression metric. If a building is not required for your next Furnace level, it can wait.

Joining the first alliance that invites you. An inactive alliance provides almost no benefits. Take two minutes to check whether the alliance has active R4/R5, recent event coordination and members online. That two minutes is worth weeks of better progression.

Spending speed-ups on short timers. Speed-ups are among the scarcest F2P resources in the game. Using them on 5-minute timers wastes their value. Save every speed-up for long Furnace upgrades and future Hall of Chiefs event windows.

Missing Bear Hunt in your first week. Bear Hunt opens early and is your first consistent source of Chief Gear materials. Missing it in week one starts a gear gap that compounds over months. Participate every session from the first one available.

Letting coal run dry. If coal hits zero, your Furnace shuts down, survivors freeze, production halts and you lose time you cannot recover. Monitor coal daily and lower Furnace power when not actively playing. It takes 30 seconds and prevents the single biggest early-game disaster.

Investing heavily in Bahiti long-term. Bahiti is a useful Gen 1 placeholder but is quickly outclassed by Molly and later heroes. Put enough into her to be functional in Exploration and Crazy Joe defence, but do not pour shards or skill manuals into a hero you will replace within your first month.
