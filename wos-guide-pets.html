Pets System:
Complete F2P Guide

Pets are passive companions that boost your city, combat and events permanently. There are 12 pets in the game, each with a different skill and priority depending on your role and stage of server. This guide covers all 12, when they unlock, which to prioritise first and how to use your Refinement materials without wasting them.

Overview

How the Pets System Works

Pets are companions that provide passive bonuses across your city, combat and event performance. Unlike heroes, pets affect your whole base rather than individual marches. The system unlocks when your state reaches ~Day 53 and your Furnace reaches Level 18 — you also need to construct the Beast Cage. There are currently 12 pets across two categories: Development pets (city growth, stamina, gathering) and Combat pets (attack, defence, capacity).

Pets have five rarity tiers: Common, Uncommon, Rare, Epic and Legendary. Higher rarity generally means stronger passive bonuses, but the specific skills matter more than rarity alone. Pets are upgraded with Pet Food, advanced through milestone levels every 10 levels, and fine-tuned through Pet Refinement using Wild Marks.

Key mechanic: Pets have active skills that trigger manually and passive stat bonuses that apply permanently. The active skills are powerful but temporary. The passive bonuses are always on and compound over time as you refine the pet. For most F2P players, the passive bonuses are what matter most in the long run.

All 12 Pets

Complete Pet Breakdown

Development Pets

These pets provide bonuses that help your city grow faster, gather more efficiently and keep your Chief active. Most F2P players prioritise at least one development pet early before transitioning to combat pets.

Cave Hyena First Pet Available

Active skill increases construction speed by ~15% for 5 minutes. F2P community consensus places this as a top early priority — faster construction means faster Furnace progression which gates almost everything else. Recommended by many F2P players as the first pet to max. Unlock requirement: Musk Ox at Level 15.

Arctic Wolf

Active skill restores ~60 Chief stamina on activation. Stamina is essential for Bear Hunt, Polar Terror and other event participation. High value for players who run stamina-heavy event schedules. F2P community rates this as a strong early investment alongside Hyena.

Musk Ox

Active skill instantly completes gathering from one resource tile. Saves significant wait time on gathering missions. Also gates Cave Hyena — you need Musk Ox at Level 15 to unlock Hyena, making it a practical early priority regardless of its direct value.

Giant Tapir

Active skill provides ~500 Pet Food on activation. Pet Food is the primary upgrade resource for all pets, making Tapir a force multiplier for your entire pet roster. Experienced players note that while 500 food sounds small, over time it accelerates other pets by several weeks. Worth developing steadily.

Giant Elk

Active skill rewards a random rare item which can include Chief Charm materials or Fire Crystals. Useful for supplementing your gear crafting and Fire Crystal progression. Value varies by luck but consistently provides something worthwhile.

Combat Pets

Combat pets provide offensive and defensive bonuses for marches, rallies and events. These become the primary focus from the mid game onwards as PvP and alliance events intensify.

Snow Ape Top Combat Priority

Active skill increases squad (march) capacity by ~15,000 for 2 hours. More troops per march means more damage per rally, more SvS points per action and a bigger contribution to every alliance event. Considered the top combat pet priority by most experienced players. Rally leaders especially should develop this first.

Iron Rhino Top Combat Priority

Active skill increases rally capacity by ~150,000 for 2 hours. The single largest rally capacity boost in the game. Essential for rally leaders in SvS, Foundry Battle and Fortress Battle. Even as a rally filler, Iron Rhino's passive defence bonuses are strong. High priority alongside Snow Ape once both are available.

Cave Lion

Active skill increases all troop attack power by ~10% for 2 hours. A direct offensive boost that applies to your whole march. Strong for offensive event play and SvS combat phases. Secondary priority after Snow Ape and Iron Rhino for most players.

Snow Leopard

Active skill increases march speed by ~30% and reduces enemy troop lethality by ~5% for 2 hours. Strong for offensive players who prioritise rapid repositioning in Foundry Battle and SvS. The speed boost is particularly useful for reaching objectives quickly.

Titan Roc

Active skill reduces enemy health by ~5% for 2 hours. Effective across PvP, Beast Hunting, rallies and Crazy Joe. A consistent debuff pet that works in most combat scenarios. Mid-tier priority for most F2P players.

Sabre-tooth Tiger

Boosts troop lethality. Strong in direct combat scenarios where dealing final damage matters. Works well alongside Iron Rhino for players who lead or participate heavily in combat rallies.

Mammoth

Late-game combat pet unlocking around Day 370. Provides strong combat buffs. Detailed community evaluation is still ongoing at this stage. A lower priority for most players until the earlier combat pets are well-developed.

Decision Framework

Which Pet Is Right For You?

Your role in the alliance and your primary activities should drive which pets you prioritise. Use this framework as a starting point:

If you are a rally leader (R3, R4 or R5):

Snow Ape first, Iron Rhino second. Rally size directly determines your damage output and SvS point generation. Every ~15,000 extra capacity from Snow Ape and ~150,000 from Iron Rhino compounds across every rally you lead.

If you are a rally filler or casual PvP participant:

Snow Ape remains first — more troops contributed per join still matters. Then focus on one offensive combat pet (Cave Lion or Titan Roc) based on your preferred events.

If you are early game F2P (Day 0 to ~Day 150):

Cave Hyena first for construction speed, Arctic Wolf for stamina if events are your focus. Combat pets are not yet available — develop Musk Ox to Level 15 to unlock Hyena and get the system moving. Save Pet Food and Refinement materials rather than spending them on early weak pets.

If you want to maximise F2P long-term efficiency:

Follow the F2P community path: Hyena to max, Wolf to max, Tapir to max. The construction speed and stamina benefits in the early game compound significantly. Then transition to Snow Ape and Iron Rhino when combat pets unlock.

Refinement Strategy

How to Use Wild Marks Efficiently

Pet Refinement fine-tunes your pet's passive stat bonuses using Common and Advanced Wild Marks. It is separate from Pet Food levelling. Refinement is also a scoring category during SvS Preparation Phase, which changes how and when you should spend your marks.

SvS Refinement rule: Save your Refinement materials and spend them specifically during the SvS Prep Phase scoring window. The scoring multiplier during this window means the same resources provide more value than spending casually throughout the week. Using materials outside of SvS is leaving event points on the table.

The key principle for Refinement is the same as other limited resources in WOS: go deep on one pet rather than spreading marks across several. A single well-refined pet outperforms multiple weakly-refined pets in almost every scenario. Pick your primary pet and focus all Refinement there until it is substantially developed before branching out.

Be aware of diminishing returns at higher refinement levels. Early refinement levels provide the highest percentage gain per material spent. As a pet approaches maximum refinement, each incremental improvement costs significantly more for less gain. This is not a reason to stop — the cumulative total still matters — but it is why going deep on one pet early is more efficient than trying to max multiple pets simultaneously.

Sources of Refinement materials (F2P): Beast Slay event, Alliance Mobilisation, world map gathering nodes, and certain event reward tracks. These are limited so treat them as a resource to be planned, not spent casually.

Common Mistakes

What Slows Your Pet Progression

Spreading Pet Food and Refinement materials across too many pets. This is the most common mistake. Spreading thin means no single pet reaches the levels where its bonuses make a meaningful difference. Pick one and develop it properly before moving on.

Refining pets outside SvS prep windows. Refinement is a scored SvS activity. Spending your Wild Marks casually during the week rather than saving them for the SvS prep window wastes scoring potential. The difference over a season compounds significantly.

Skipping Musk Ox development early. Musk Ox needs to reach Level 15 to unlock Cave Hyena. Players who ignore Musk Ox delay unlocking one of the most valuable early F2P pets. Get Musk Ox to Level 15 as a gating priority even if you do not plan to develop it further.

Waiting for Snow Ape before investing in any pets. Snow Ape unlocks around Day 280. Players who invest nothing in pets before that point miss ~280 days of compounding development pet value. Cave Hyena and Arctic Wolf provide meaningful early gains that contribute to long-term progression.

Confusing maxing level with maxing percentages. A pet at maximum level is not fully developed. The percentage bonuses from Refinement take considerably longer to max than the level cap. Players sometimes assume a max-level pet is complete — the refinement percentage grind continues well beyond level cap.
