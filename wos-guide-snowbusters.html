Snowbusters:
Complete Event
Guide

Snowbusters looks simple: drive a snowplow, clear snow. The depth is in resource management. Get the gear upgrade order right, move in one consistent direction and you will clear significantly more efficiently than players who just roam the map.

Event Guide ✓ Updated April 2026
⚡ Quick Summary
Event Type
Solo snowplow clearing, 3 resources
Gear Priority
Gloves, Coat, Snowbuster, Boots
Core Rule
One direction. No random roaming.
Biggest Mistake
Upgrading gear in the wrong order
The Three Resources

Fuel, Health and Coal

⛽
Fuel
Consumed by movement, snow clearing and fighting. Starts at 200. Regenerates at 12 every 5 minutes. Never waste it on the last 5% of snow in an area. Use the rocket at 95% cleared.
❤
Health
Drops when fighting enemies or taking damage. Zero health means you drop all your Coal. Manage health via the central fire and medkits. Stay close to fire when possible.
💨
Coal
Used to upgrade the Fire Pit each stage. Leftover coal converts to bonus fuel at stage end. Only upgrade the Fire Pit when necessary. Save the rest for the bonus fuel conversion.
Gear Upgrade Order

Upgrade in This Exact Order

Gear resets after every Snowbusters event. The goal is to invest your Toolkits in the order that gives the most benefit during the current event. This is the F2P priority order:

1
Gloves
Boosts health and reduces fuel consumption. Directly extends how long you can keep clearing snow. The most impactful early upgrade for F2P players.
2
Coat
Improves damage resistance and determines how fast your Fuel recovers. A higher Coat level gives you more total Fuel across the entire event. Upgrade this on Day 1 if possible for maximum effect across all three event days.
3
Snowbuster
Increases attack power and snow-clearing range. Useful but lower priority than the survival-focused upgrades above. Upgrade once Gloves and Coat are handled.
4
Boots
Increases movement speed and improves fire regeneration. Genuinely useful but the last priority. The other three pieces affect your core loop more directly.

Coat is the key to Fuel recovery. The level of your Coat directly determines how fast your Fuel regenerates and how much total Fuel you get during the whole event. Upgrading Coat to maximum level on Day 1 pays dividends across all three runs. Every subsequent session will have more fuel available than if you upgraded it later.

Clearing Strategy

How to Clear Efficiently

Clear in one consistent direction

Pick a direction at the start of each run and maintain it. Random roaming wastes Fuel on backtracking, leaves incomplete patches behind you and dramatically reduces total snow cleared per session compared to a consistent directional approach.

Use the rocket at 95% cleared, not before

The rocket finishes a snow area when it reaches 95% cleared. Using it earlier wastes its impact. Using it at exactly 95% is the most Fuel-efficient way to complete each area and move on to the next one.

Teleport to fresh areas rather than waiting for respawns

When your current area is cleared, use the event teleport to jump to a new dense snow area immediately. Time spent waiting for snow to respawn is time your Fuel is not converting into cleared area and score.

Upgrade the Fire Pit only when necessary

Every Fire Pit upgrade costs Coal. The leftover Coal at stage end converts into bonus Fuel. Only upgrade the Fire Pit when your Health is dangerously low and you need the regeneration. Save Coal for the end-stage Fuel conversion otherwise.

Snowmen and Beasts

How to Handle Enemies

Not all enemies are worth engaging. Fighting costs Fuel and Health. The value equation determines whether to fight or avoid:

Snowmen: Engage when they are directly blocking your clearing path or when the reward justifies the Fuel cost. Weaker snowmen in the early stages are usually worth defeating to clear path efficiently. Stronger ones late in the event may not be worth the resource drain.

Beasts: Avoid unless the reward is specifically needed. Beasts deal significant Health damage and cost more Fuel to defeat than snowmen. Routing around them is almost always the better choice unless they are sitting on a high-value snow clearing area you cannot bypass.

Common Mistakes

What Most Players Get Wrong

Roaming randomly across the map. Random movement wastes Fuel on backtracking and leaves incomplete clearing areas. Clearing in one consistent direction is significantly more efficient and produces meaningfully better scores.

Upgrading gear in the wrong order. Skipping Coat to upgrade Snowbuster first is one of the most common Snowbusters mistakes. Coat determines your total Fuel availability across all three days. Getting it wrong on Day 1 cannot be fully recovered.

Using the rocket too early. The rocket should be used at 95% cleared. Using it at 70% or 80% wastes the rocket on an area that still has significant remaining snow. Wait for the 95% threshold every time.

Upgrading the Fire Pit too frequently. Every unnecessary Fire Pit upgrade is Coal that does not convert into bonus Fuel at stage end. Only upgrade when Health genuinely needs the regeneration. Otherwise hold the Coal.
