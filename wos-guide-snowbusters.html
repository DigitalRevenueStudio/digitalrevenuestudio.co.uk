Snowbusters:
Complete Event
Guide

Snowbusters looks simple: drive a snowplow, clear snow. The depth is in resource management. Get the gear upgrade order right, move in one consistent direction and you will earn significantly better rewards than players who just roam the map randomly.

Event Guide ✓ Updated April 2026
⚡ Quick Summary
Three Resources
Fuel, Health, Coal
Gear Priority
Gloves, then Coat, then Snowbuster, then Boots

Roaming randomly and wasting Fuel on movement
The Three Resources

Fuel, Health and Coal

⛽
Fuel

Consumed by movement, snow clearing and fighting. Starts at 200. Regenerates at 12 every 5 minutes. Runs out means you stop. Never waste it on the last 5% of snow in any area.

❤
Health

Drops when fighting enemies or taking damage. Zero health means you drop all your Coal. Manage health via the central fire and medkits. Stay close to fire when possible.

💨
Coal

Used to upgrade the Fire Pit each stage. Leftover coal converts to bonus fuel at stage end. Only upgrade the Fire Pit when necessary and save the rest for conversion.

Gear Upgrade Order

Upgrade in This Exact Order

Gear resets after every Snowbusters event so there is no carrying upgrades forward. The goal is to invest your Toolkits in the order that gives the most benefit during the current run.

1
Gloves
Boosts health and reduces fuel consumption. Directly extends how long you can keep clearing snow. The most impactful early upgrade for F2P players.
2
Coat
Improves damage resistance and determines how fast your Fuel recovers. A higher Coat level gives you more total Fuel across the entire event. Upgrade this on Day 1 if possible for maximum effect. This is the key upgrade for sustained performance.
3
Snowbuster
Increases attack power and snow-clearing range. Useful but lower priority than the survival-focused upgrades above. Upgrade once Gloves and Coat are handled.
4
Boots
Increases movement speed and improves fire regeneration. Genuinely useful but the last priority. The other three gear pieces affect your core loop more directly.

Coat is the key to fuel recovery. The level of your Coat directly determines how fast Fuel regenerates and how much total Fuel you get during the whole event. Upgrading Coat to maximum on Day 1 pays dividends across all three days. Every subsequent run will have more fuel available than if you upgraded it later.

Clearing Strategy

How to Clear Efficiently

➡
Always clear in one direction. Moving systematically in one consistent direction is the most fuel-efficient clearing method. Zigzagging and backtracking wastes Fuel on movement without clearing new snow. Pick a direction at the start of each stage and stick to it.
🧍
Rescue survivors first. Each survivor you rescue increases your snow-clearing radius by 10%. Survivors near the Fire Pit should be your first priority when entering a new stage. They make every subsequent action more efficient.
🎯
Stop at 95% and use the rocket. Once you hit 95% snow cleared, use the rocket to finish the stage. Never waste Fuel clearing the last 5% manually. The rocket activates at 95% and handles the remainder instantly.
🔥
Stay close to the central fire. The fire regenerates your health passively when nearby. Routing your clearing path to keep you within range reduces medkit usage and keeps your Health stable throughout the stage.
⚔
Be selective about which enemies you fight. Some enemies cost more Fuel and Health than the reward justifies. Prioritise enemies that drop useful items and avoid extended fights with low-value targets that drain your resources.
Common Mistakes

What Most Players Get Wrong

Roaming randomly across the map. Clearing in one consistent direction is significantly more efficient than roaming. Random movement wastes Fuel, leaves incomplete areas and reduces your total clear count by the end of the event.

Upgrading gear in the wrong order. The gear upgrade order determines your clearing efficiency for the entire event. Not upgrading Coat early means every run has less total Fuel than it should. This compounds across all three days of the event.

Not using the rocket at 95%. The rocket finishes the final 5% instantly without Fuel cost. Using it too early wastes its clearing power. Clearing the last 5% manually wastes Fuel inefficiently. Use it exactly at 95%.

Not rescuing survivors promptly. Every survivor adds 10% to your clearing radius. Ignoring them and clearing snow in the wrong order means every subsequent action is less efficient than it should be.

Fighting every enemy regardless of reward. Some enemies are worth engaging, others cost more Fuel and Health than the reward justifies. Assess each enemy before engaging and skip those that drain resources without meaningful gain.
