Crazy Joe:
Complete Alliance
Event Guide

Crazy Joe is 40 minutes, 20 waves and one of the most reliable sources of Chief Gear upgrade materials in the game. This guide covers troop strategy, hero setup, the wave breakdown and how to maximise your score as F2P.

How It Works

The Core Mechanics

Crazy Joe sends waves of bandits attacking every alliance member's city simultaneously. Your job is to defend your own city and reinforce your allies. Both earn points. Shields do not work during this event.

How points are calculated: Points are based on the percentage of attackers you defeat. 100% of a wave killed equals maximum points. The city owner earns points regardless of whether their own troops or alliance reinforcements do the killing. Reinforcing allies earns roughly 50% of the rate of defending your own city, but it is still worth doing consistently throughout the event.

Troop Strategy

What to Send, What to Keep

✓ Send Out
✓
All Infantry — send to nearby allies first. Infantry fight first in turn order and contribute the most kills in outgoing marches.
✓
All Lancers — send once your Infantry marches are full. Use every available march queue.
✓
Prioritise online allies first. Waves 7, 14 and 17 target active players for double points. Reinforcing online members during these waves benefits everyone.
✕ Keep at Home
✕
All Marksmen — keep every Marksman in your base. They rarely contribute kills in outgoing marches and are significantly more valuable defending your own city.
✕
Your 3 strongest heroes — keep them at home on defence. Heroes use Expedition skills during city defence and provide a significant boost to how many bandits your troops eliminate.
✕
Do not use your strongest attack heroes in outgoing squads. Use defence-focused heroes like Patrick and Sergey as squad leads when sending troops to allies. Their damage reduction skills keep your reinforcement troops alive longer.

Advanced: the zero defence strategy. Players above Furnace 26 sometimes send out all Infantry and Lancers, leaving only Marksmen at home and relying entirely on alliance reinforcements to defend. This requires tight coordination and pre-arranged cover from online allies. Only attempt this if your alliance has confirmed they will reinforce you.

Hero Setup

Which Heroes to Use Where

🏠
Defending your city: Keep your 3 strongest combat heroes at home. Their Expedition skills activate during city defence and make a significant difference in wave survival. Avoid leaving Growth heroes like Zinman on home defence as they contribute almost nothing in this context.
🦵
Sending squads to allies: Use defence-focused heroes like Patrick and Sergey as the first hero in each outgoing squad. Their damage reduction skills keep your troops alive longer at allied cities, which translates directly into more kills and more points for both you and the ally you are defending.
⭐
Baldur Expert tip: Baldur's skills provide extra event-related gains during Crazy Joe. If you have Baldur developed, make sure he is active during the event. This is one of the less obvious multipliers that most players miss.
Wave Breakdown

The 20 Waves — What to Expect

Most waves target all alliance cities simultaneously. Three elite waves target online players only for double points. Two HQ waves target the Alliance Headquarters directly.

1–6
All alliance cities
Standard waves. Pre-send reinforcements before Wave 1 so they are already defending when it hits.
7 Elite
Online players only
Double points. Be online. Alert your alliance to be active for this wave. Reinforcing online members here is especially valuable.
8–9
All alliance cities
Continue reinforcing. Check who is struggling and prioritise them with fresh marches.
10 HQ
Alliance HQ only
High value wave. Recall nearby troops immediately and reinforce HQ. Your highest-capacity player should be defending HQ for this wave.
11–13
All alliance cities
Re-send troops after the HQ wave. Marches should be back from allies by now — resend immediately.
14 Elite
Online players only
Double points. Second elite wave. Coordinate alliance availability in advance and ensure as many members as possible are online.
15–16
All alliance cities
Keep reinforcements active. Check alliance chat for members who need help.
17 Elite
Online players only
Double points. Third and final elite wave. The most important one to be online for. Being online for all three elite waves is the single biggest scoring lever in the event.
18–19
All alliance cities
Final push. Keep all marches active until the event ends.
20 HQ
Alliance HQ only
Survival wave. Focus on keeping HQ standing. Use any remaining buffs if needed.
Preparation

Before the Event Starts

💬
Coordinate with your alliance. Agree a start time, confirm who is covering HQ for Waves 10 and 20, and make sure members know to be online for Waves 7, 14 and 17.
📨
Pre-send reinforcements before Wave 1. Troops sent after Wave 1 starts arrive late. Pre-position your Infantry and Lancers at allied cities before the event begins.
⚡
Activate buffs and pets. Any troop attack, defence or health boost should go up before the event starts. Even small percentage differences compound across 20 waves.
🚫
Drop your Shield before the event. Shields block allied reinforcements from reaching your city. Remove it before Crazy Joe starts so allies can reinforce you during the event.
🚫
Do not teleport during the event. Teleporting cancels your participation for that wave segment. Stay in position throughout.
Common Mistakes

What Most Players Get Wrong

Sending Marksmen to allies. This is the most common Crazy Joe mistake. Marksmen are your best home defenders and their value defending your city far exceeds their contribution in outgoing marches. Keep every Marksman home without exception.

Not enabling Auto-Join before the event. Auto-Join allows your troops to automatically reinforce rallies at allied cities while you manage your own defence. Without it you miss a significant portion of available points. Enable it in settings before the event opens.

Using a Shield during the event. Shields block all alliance reinforcements from reaching your city. This leaves you defending solo while your alliance watches. Drop your shield before Crazy Joe starts.

Not being online for Waves 7, 14 and 17. These three elite waves target online players only and award double points. Missing all three elite waves means leaving the biggest scoring windows of the entire event unclaimed.

Leaving Growth heroes like Zinman on home defence. Growth heroes have weak combat Expedition skills and provide almost no defence value during Crazy Joe waves. Put your strongest combat heroes on home defence and rotate Zinman out before the event starts.
