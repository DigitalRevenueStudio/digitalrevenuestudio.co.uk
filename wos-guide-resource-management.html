📦 Resource Strategy

Resource Management
for F2P Players

Every building upgrade, every troop trained, every research completed requires resources. Managing them well is not glamorous, but it is the difference between consistent progression and constantly being resource-blocked. This guide covers the habits that compound.

The Four Resources

What Each Resource Does

🔥
Coal

The most critical resource. Powers your Furnace. If it runs out, survivors freeze, get sick and stop working, halting all production. Never let coal run dry. Monitor it daily.

Key habit: Do not run Furnace at Max Power constantly. Use manual controls. Lower power during warm cycles, Max only during storms and cold nights.
🌳
Wood

Primary building resource. Almost every structure and upgrade requires large amounts of wood. Usually abundant early but becomes a bottleneck during mass upgrades.

Key habit: Keep the Sawmill upgraded and staffed. Send gathering marches to wood nodes whenever march queues are free.
🍖
Meat

Feeds survivors and troops. Troop training is a heavy meat consumer. Running low slows production, makes survivors unhappy and stalls training queues.

Key habit: Keep the Kitchen and Hunter's Hut fully staffed. Assign gathering heroes to Meat nodes when not needed elsewhere.
⚙
Iron

Advanced buildings and equipment. Less critical early, increasingly important for mid and late-game upgrades especially troop and Chief Gear progression.

Key habit: Iron nodes on the world map are harder to stockpile from base production. Prioritise iron gathering in the mid game.
Production Habits

Making Your City Work Harder

👤
Always staff your production buildings. Coal Mine, Kitchen, Sawmill and Iron Mine need workers assigned to run at full capacity. Check survivor assignments regularly. Idle buildings are lost production you cannot recover.
📈
Upgrade production buildings whenever possible. Even one level on the Coal Mine or Sawmill increases output permanently. Prioritise these alongside Furnace upgrades rather than leaving them under-levelled.
🌡
Manage Furnace power manually. Running Max Power constantly burns through coal at an unsustainable rate. Lower power during warmer daytime cycles. Activate Max Power during blizzards and cold nights only. This single habit saves thousands of coal per day.
📦
Keep resource crates sealed until you are ready to spend. Crates in your inventory cannot be raided. Open resources sitting in your city are a visible target. Only open crates when you are immediately ready to use them on an upgrade or training queue.
Gathering Strategy

The World Map Is Your Main Supply Chain

Base production alone is never enough beyond the early game. Gathering from resource nodes on the world map is the primary way to stockpile what you need for high-level upgrades and sustained troop training.

🌃
The overnight gather habit. Before going offline, send all marches to resource nodes. A full night of gathering while you are away produces more resources than most players generate during their active session. Never leave march queues empty overnight.
🎯
Target higher-level nodes. Level 3 and above resource nodes yield significantly more per march than lower-level ones. Prioritise these when available, even if they are slightly further away on the map.
🦸
Use dedicated gathering heroes. Heroes with Gathering Speed skills dramatically increase yield per march. Assign your best gathering heroes specifically to resource marches rather than leaving them on your combat formation.
🛡
Gather inside alliance territory when possible. Alliance territory nodes often provide a speed bonus and reduced risk of disruption. Position your city inside the alliance zone and prioritise internal territory nodes for your gathering marches.

Protection habit: Large unshielded resource stockpiles are a visible target for attackers. Either spend resources on upgrades and training regularly or ensure a shield is active when going offline with significant reserves. Never hoard without protection.

Common Mistakes

What Most Players Get Wrong

Running Furnace at maximum power constantly. Max power burns coal rapidly. Lower the Furnace power setting when not actively playing. This single habit saves thousands of coal per day and prevents the production collapse that happens when coal runs out overnight.

Not sending gathering marches consistently. Gathering marches work while you are offline. Queuing full march capacity on world map nodes before going offline every night is one of the highest passive-value habits in the game. Empty march queues overnight are wasted time.

Opening resource crates without a plan to spend them. Once opened, resources sit in your city and attract attackers. Only open crates when you are immediately ready to use them. Sealed crates in your inventory are safe from raids.

Not staffing production buildings. Unstaffed buildings produce nothing. Check survivor assignments regularly, especially after building upgrades which can reset staffing. Idle Coal Mines and Sawmills are a consistent resource leak.

Gathering from low-level nodes when higher ones are available. Higher-level world map nodes provide significantly better yield per march. The extra time to reach a Level 3 or 4 node is almost always worth it compared to farming Level 1 nodes nearby.
