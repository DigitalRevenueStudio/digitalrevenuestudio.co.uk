📦 Resource Strategy

Resource Management
for F2P Players

Every building upgrade, every troop trained, every research completed requires resources. Managing them well is not glamorous but it is the difference between consistent progression and constantly being resource-blocked.

The Four Resources

What Each Resource Does

🔥 Coal
The most critical resource. Powers your Furnace. If it runs out, survivors freeze, get sick and stop working, halting all production across your city. Never let coal run dry. Monitor it daily.
Key habit: Do not run Furnace at Max Power constantly. Use manual controls: lower power during warm daytime cycles, Max Power only during blizzards and cold nights. This single habit saves thousands of coal per day.
🌳 Wood
Primary building resource. Almost every structure and upgrade requires large amounts of wood. Usually abundant early but becomes a bottleneck during mass upgrades.
Key habit: Keep the Sawmill upgraded and fully staffed. Send gathering marches to wood nodes whenever marches are free.
🍖 Meat
Feeds your survivors and troops. Troop training is a heavy meat consumer. Running out slows production, makes survivors unhappy and stalls training queues.
Key habit: Keep the Kitchen and Hunter's Hut fully staffed. Assign gathering heroes to Meat nodes when not needed elsewhere.
⚙ Iron
Advanced buildings and equipment. Less critical early but increasingly important for mid and late-game upgrades, especially troop and Chief Gear progression.
Key habit: Iron nodes on the world map are harder to stockpile from base production alone. Prioritise iron gathering in the mid game and keep the Iron Mine levelled.
Production Habits

Making Your City Work Harder

👤
Always staff your production buildings

Coal Mine, Kitchen, Sawmill and Iron Mine need workers assigned to run at full capacity. Check your survivor assignments regularly. Idle buildings are lost production you cannot recover.

📈
Upgrade production buildings consistently

Even one level on the Coal Mine or Sawmill increases output permanently. Prioritise these alongside Furnace upgrades rather than leaving them under-levelled for weeks.

🌡
Manage Furnace power manually

Running Max Power constantly burns coal at an unsustainable rate. Lower power during warmer daytime cycles. Activate Max Power only during blizzards and cold nights. This single habit saves thousands of coal per day.

📦
Keep resource crates unopened

Crates in your inventory cannot be raided. Only open them when you are about to spend them immediately. Hoarding open resources without a Shield active makes you a visible target.

Gathering Strategy

The World Map Is Your Main Supply Chain

Passive production alone will not sustain an active F2P account. World map gathering is your primary resource supply and it works while you are offline.

The overnight gather: Before you go offline, send all your marches to resource nodes on the world map. A full night of gathering while you sleep can provide more total resources than several hours of active play. Never go offline with idle marches.

🎯
Target higher-level nodes

Level 3 and Level 4 resource nodes yield significantly more per march than Level 1 or 2 nodes. Always prioritise the highest-level node available to you, even if it means marching further.

🛡
Gather inside alliance territory when possible

Alliance territory nodes often provide a speed bonus and are better protected from attack. Position your city within the alliance zone and prioritise territory nodes over unprotected open-world nodes.

Gathering Heroes

Assign the Right Heroes to Gather

Gathering heroes have high Gathering Speed and Carrying Capacity in their Exploration stats. Using combat heroes for gathering is a common mistake: they fill the march slot but gather significantly less per trip than dedicated gathering heroes.

Key gathering heroes to develop: Gina increases gathering speed and march capacity significantly. Yuka and other gathering-stat heroes provide similar benefits. Check your heroes' Exploration stats before assigning them to gathering marches.

Protecting What You Have

Keep Raiders Out

✓ Do
Use a Shield before going offline with large stockpiles
Keep resource crates in inventory until you need them
Spend resources on upgrades and training promptly
Stay within alliance territory for passive protection
Join an active alliance with a strong NAP agreement
✕ Do Not
Hoard large open stockpiles without a Shield active
Open resource crates long before you plan to use them
Leave your city outside alliance territory overnight
Sit on resources while Furnace upgrades are available
Ignore resource gathering while marches are idle
Common Mistakes

What Most Players Get Wrong

Running Furnace at maximum power constantly. Max Power burns coal rapidly. This is the most common cause of coal shortages in the early and mid game. Lower the Furnace power setting when you are not actively playing and coal will almost never run dry.

Not sending gathering marches before going offline. Idle marches overnight are one of the biggest passive resource losses in the game. The habit of sending all marches to world map nodes before closing the app compounds significantly over weeks.

Hoarding open resources without a Shield. Large unshielded stockpiles are visible targets. Either spend resources on upgrades and training promptly, or keep a Shield active when going offline with significant stockpiles.

Gathering from low-level nodes when higher ones are available. Level 1 and 2 nodes provide a fraction of the yield of Level 3 and 4 nodes for the same march time. Always prioritise the highest-level accessible node.

Not staffing production buildings. Unstaffed buildings produce nothing. Coal Mine running at half capacity because survivors were not assigned is one of the most preventable resource bottlenecks. Check building assignments weekly at minimum.
