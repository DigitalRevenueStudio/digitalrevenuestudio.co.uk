Server Timeline
Complete Guide

Every server in Whiteout Survival follows the same progression path. New heroes, pets, systems and events unlock at specific points on that timeline. Knowing where your server sits, what is coming next and how to prepare for each unlock is one of the most important strategic advantages available to any player, F2P or otherwise.

Finding Your Server Age

How Old Is Your Server?

All timing in this guide is measured from Day 0, the day your server was created. Knowing your server age lets you predict upcoming unlocks and plan your resource spending around them. There are three ways to calculate it, all of which give estimates rather than exact dates.

01
VIP Login Days
The most accurate method if you joined the server shortly after launch and have logged in every single day. Open your VIP panel and check your Login Days total. That number is a close estimate of your server age in days. Any missed logins reduce accuracy.
02
Monument Beast Hunting Task
Open your Monument and find the Beast Hunting task. Note the date it was unlocked, then subtract 6 days. That gives your estimated server creation date. Use any online date calculator to find the number of days since then.
03
Sunfire Castle Timing
For older servers that have had at least one Sunfire Battle. Open Sunfire Castle, go to Battle Record, then Hall of Fame. Find the date of the first President. Add 54 days to get your estimated server creation date, then count forward from there.

All methods are estimates. Timing varies between servers depending on activity levels, game updates and server-specific factors. Treat any date as approximate within a range of roughly 7 to 21 days. Senior alliance members on your server are often the best source of confirmation.

Common Mistakes

What Loses You Weeks of Progress

Spending Gold Shards on Gen 1 heroes just before Gen 2 unlocks. Gold Shards spent on Molly or Sergey in the week before Alonso arrives is one of the most painful and avoidable F2P mistakes. Check your server age before every major resource spend.

Spending Gold Shards on Flint. Flint reaches 5 stars through Lucky Wheel spins alone during Hall of Chiefs Day 2. Gold Shards belong on Alonso. This is one of the most common costly errors in the early game.

Investing Exclusive Gear in short-lifespan heroes. Exclusive Gear takes weeks to accumulate. Only invest it in heroes you are confident will stay in your lineup for multiple generations. Alonso, Mia, Logan and Wayne are the safest targets.

Neglecting Furnace upgrades. Most events, unlocks and progression systems are gated behind Furnace level. Falling behind on Furnace upgrades creates bottlenecks that compound across every other system.

Ignoring War Academy when it unlocks at Day 220. The power difference between players with a developed War Academy and those who ignore it is substantial and grows over time. Prioritise Books of Knowledge investment immediately at this milestone.

Skipping pet investment early because impact feels small. Pets compound over time. Players who start investing at Day 53 have a significant advantage by Day 200 compared to those who deprioritise pets entirely in the early game.

Action Plan

What Should I Do Right Now?

Find your current server stage below and follow the priority list for your situation.

Early Server (Gen 1 to 2, Day 0 to ~85)
→ Furnace first. Push toward Furnace 16 for Foundry Battle unlock.
→ Build Molly and Sergey. Solid F2P foundation that carries through Gen 2.
→ Save Gold Shards. Do not spend on any Gen 1 hero if Gen 2 is within a few weeks.
→ Join an active top-5 alliance immediately. Alliance events are your primary resource source at this stage.
→ Save speed-ups for Hall of Chiefs. Do not spend them on short timers.
→ Redeem gift codes. Free resources from Day 1. Check the tracker regularly.
Mid Server (Gen 3 to 5, Day ~85 to ~360)
→ Alonso to 4 stars. Your Gold Shard target from Gen 2 onwards.
→ Start building Mia. Long-term Lancer investment relevant into Gen 6 and beyond.
→ Complete every SvS prep phase daily task. Consistent participation separates competitive players from casual ones.
→ Prioritise War Academy at Day 220. It is the biggest power jump on the entire timeline. Do not delay.
→ Invest in Iron Rhino and Snow Ape pets. Defensive contribution and march capacity respectively.
→ Check next generation timing. If it is within 2 weeks, switch to saving mode for Gold Shards and Gems.
Late Server (Gen 6+, Day 360+)
→ Target Wayne. The F2P endgame infantry hero. Alonso, Mia and Wayne is the competitive late-game F2P lineup.
→ Focus Fire Crystal progression. The primary late-game power lever.
→ Alliance coordination is everything. Individual power matters less than strategic alliance play at this stage.
→ Consolidate hero investments. Depth over breadth. Max your core lineup before expanding.
→ Alliance Championship structure buffs compound. Holding structures creates advantages that carry across every other event.

The core F2P rule across all stages: Focus on 1 to 2 heroes per generation. Use Lucky Wheel during featured Hall of Chiefs events for wheel heroes. Save Gold Shards for Alonso then the next confirmed long-term hero. Never spend Gems on speed-ups or resource packs.
