Speed-Ups:
When to Use
When to Wait

Speed-ups are the most misused resource in Whiteout Survival. New players burn them on five-minute timers and wonder why they fall behind. This guide explains exactly when to use them, when to save them, and which events make them most valuable.

Types of Speed-Ups

Know What You Have

🏗
Construction
Reduces building upgrade timers. Your most common type. Highest value used on long Furnace upgrades or during Hall of Chiefs Day 1.
🔬
Research
Reduces research timers only. Save for Hall of Chiefs Day 4 or SvS research days. Using them outside events wastes their scoring potential.
⚔
Training
Reduces troop training timers. Save for Hall of Chiefs Day 3 and SvS training days. Outside events, only use to keep queues from stalling overnight.
⚡
General
Can be used for any timer. Your most flexible resource. Save as a buffer for unexpected long timers or event days where you run short of a specific type.

The core mistake: Using speed-ups on short timers feels satisfying but is the slowest path forward. A 5-minute timer burned with a 1-hour speed-up wastes 55 minutes of potential. Short timers complete naturally. Only use speed-ups when the timer is long or the event demands it.

Event Value

Where Speed-Ups Score The Most Points

The highest-value use of any speed-up is during an event that scores points for using them. The same speed-up provides time benefit plus event points during the right window, versus time benefit only outside it. That difference can mean hitting a milestone reward or missing it entirely.

The Rules

Speed-Up Rules for F2P

Only use speed-ups on timers over 8 hours outside events

Outside of Hall of Chiefs and SvS windows, a good starting rule is: only burn a speed-up if the timer is 8 hours or more and there is a specific reason to finish sooner. Short timers complete naturally overnight.

Save construction speed-ups specifically for Hall of Chiefs Day 1

Day 1 of Hall of Chiefs is the highest-scoring construction window in the early game. Using construction speed-ups casually in the days before costs you top-tier rewards that those speed-ups would have generated.

Save research speed-ups for Hall of Chiefs Day 4 or SvS Day 5

Research speed-ups used outside these windows earn zero event points. The wait is worth it: a research speed-up used on Hall of Chiefs Day 4 provides both the time benefit and meaningful milestone progress.

Never mix up speed-up types

Construction speed-ups only work on building timers. Research speed-ups only work on research. Using the wrong type on a timer has no effect and the speed-up is consumed. Check which type you are using before applying.

Start hoarding speed-ups 2 to 3 weeks before Hall of Chiefs

The players who rank highest in Hall of Chiefs as F2P are almost always the ones who stopped using speed-ups casually the longest before the event. Start saving specifically once you know the next HoC is 2 to 3 weeks away.

Common Mistakes

What Most Players Get Wrong

Using speed-ups on short timers. This is the defining bad habit for F2P speed-up management. A 5-minute timer burned with a 1-hour speed-up wastes 55 minutes of potential that could have been applied to a long Furnace upgrade or event window. Never touch a short timer.

Using construction speed-ups before Hall of Chiefs Day 1. If you use your construction speed-up stack on a Furnace upgrade the week before Hall of Chiefs, you arrive at the event empty-handed for the highest-scoring construction window of the early game.

Not knowing which day scores which speed-up type. Hall of Chiefs Day 4 scores research. Hall of Chiefs Day 1 scores construction. Using the wrong type on the wrong day earns zero HoC points. Know the schedule before the event opens.

Spending General speed-ups on short timers to clear the queue. General speed-ups are the most versatile resource. Burning them on short timers to tidy up your build queue wastes them against the event windows where they would have provided points as well as time.
