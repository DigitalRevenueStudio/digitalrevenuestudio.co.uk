Speed-Ups:
When to Use
When to Wait

Speed-ups are the most misused resource in Whiteout Survival. New players burn them on five-minute timers and wonder why they fall behind. This guide explains exactly when to use them, when to save them and which events make them most valuable.

Resource Strategy ✓ Updated April 2026
⚡ Quick Summary
Best Window
Hall of Chiefs and SvS prep days
Overnight Exception
Long timers of 6+ hours that would sit idle
Hard Rule
Never on timers under 30 minutes

Using them casually outside event windows
Types of Speed-Ups

Know What You Have

🏗
Construction

Reduces building upgrade timers. Your most common type. Highest value used on long Furnace upgrades or during Hall of Chiefs Day 1.

🔬
Research

Reduces research timers only. Save for Hall of Chiefs Day 4 or SvS research days. Using them outside events wastes scoring potential.

⚔
Training

Reduces troop training timers. Save for Hall of Chiefs Day 3 and SvS training days. Outside events, only use to keep queues from stalling overnight.

⚡
General

Works on any timer. Your most flexible resource. Save as a buffer for unexpected long timers or event days where you run short of the specific type needed.

The core mistake: Using speed-ups on short timers feels satisfying but is the slowest path forward. A 5-minute timer you burn a 1-hour speed-up on wastes 55 minutes of potential. The habit that separates strong F2P players is simple: only use speed-ups when the timer is long or the event demands it.

The Rules

F2P Speed-Up Rules

📦
Save until an event demands them. The ideal time to spend big is Hall of Chiefs and SvS prep. Both events score points for construction, research and training speed-ups on specific days. Hold your stockpile for these windows and resist using them before.
⏰
The overnight exception. If a long Furnace upgrade of 6 hours or more would sit idle overnight, using a speed-up to complete it is acceptable. Idle queues are wasted time. This is the one exception to the save-everything rule.
🚫
Never use speed-ups on timers under 30 minutes. The time saving is negligible. Short timers will finish naturally. Save every speed-up for what matters.
🔍
Check which type before applying. Construction speed-ups only work on buildings. Research speed-ups only work on research. Using the wrong type has no effect and wastes the speed-up. Always verify the type before applying.
📋
Know the Hall of Chiefs day schedule before it starts. Each HoC day has a specific scoring category. Using research speed-ups on Day 1 (construction day) earns zero HoC points. Know the schedule before the event opens and have your stockpile ready for the right day.
Common Mistakes

What Most Players Get Wrong

Using speed-ups on short timers. A 1-hour speed-up used on a 5-minute timer wastes 55 minutes of its value. Short timers finish naturally. This is the single most common speed-up mistake and the one that most visibly holds F2P players back.

Not saving construction speed-ups for Hall of Chiefs Day 1. HoC Day 1 is the highest-scoring construction window in the early game. Using construction speed-ups casually in the days before it costs you top-tier rewards those speed-ups would have generated.

Using research speed-ups outside Hall of Chiefs Day 4 or SvS research days. Research speed-ups used outside these specific windows earn only the time benefit, not the event points. Hold them specifically for these two windows.

Mixing up speed-up types. Construction speed-ups only work on buildings. Research speed-ups only work on research. Applying the wrong type wastes it completely. Check the type label before applying to any timer.

Not stockpiling before Hall of Chiefs begins. The players who rank highest in HoC as F2P are almost always the ones who saved the longest. Every speed-up burned casually two weeks before HoC is a speed-up that scores no points during the event.
