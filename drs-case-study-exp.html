The Digital Income Experiment —
Brand build & content for an independent client

A private individual documenting their online income testing approached Digital Revenue Studio to build their brand from scratch and manage their ongoing content. Here is what we delivered.

2026Project started
9Income streams documented
OngoingContent retainer
The client

An individual documenting income testing honestly

The Digital Income Experiment is an independent project run by a private individual testing online income streams and documenting the results in real time. The brief to Digital Revenue Studio was straightforward: build a brand, build a website and help produce the content.

The client had a clear vision — an honest, no-agenda resource where real income experiment results are published openly, including the failures. No products sold, no affiliate agenda baked into the content. Just data.

“The brief was simple: build something people can trust because it has nothing to sell them.”

Our job was to give that vision a professional brand and platform, and to produce the written content that brings it to life each month. The client runs the experiments and provides the numbers. We build the strategy and write the words.

Scope of work

What we were asked to do

Brand identity

Name, visual identity, logo and brand positioning for a brand that needed to feel independent, honest and credible.

Website design and build

Full website built and hosted on GitHub Pages. Designed to be clean, fast and easy to navigate on mobile. Gated lead magnet page built to capture an email audience from day one.

Content strategy

Monthly content plan covering all active income stream experiments. Each experiment gets its own documented post — platform tested, method used, honest result published.

Ongoing blog content

We write the content. The client provides the data. Published on an ongoing retainer basis as experiments conclude and results come in.

Specialist content pieces

Including a council tax band challenge blog and free letter template, and a car finance DCA blog and complaint letter template — produced by DRS, published under the client's brand.
