SvS Prep Phase:
The 5-Day
F2P Playbook

State vs State is the biggest event in Whiteout Survival. Most players treat the prep phase as a warm-up. The players who rank consistently well treat it as the event itself. Prep phase scoring is where a large portion of individual rank is decided before battle even starts.

State vs State F2P Strategy ✓ Updated April 2026
⚡ Quick Summary
Structure
5-day prep phase, then battle phase
Core Rule
Each day has one scoring category. Wrong day earns zero.
Highest Scoring Day
Day 3: Beast Hunt
Biggest Mistake
Spending resources before prep phase opens

The core rule: Each prep day rewards a specific activity. Using research speed-ups on Day 1 instead of Day 5 scores you nothing and wastes them in the wrong window. Hold resources, then use them on the right day.

Before It Starts

Matchmaking: Before Prep Phase Opens

Before the prep phase opens there is an opportunity to influence which state you face. Matchmaking occurs on Saturday and Sunday UTC. If the top players in your state coordinate a temporary power reduction during this window (removing hero gear, resetting hero levels through Drill Camp) to influence the match in your favour.

Individual actions have minimal effect. It only works as a coordinated effort across your state's top 100 to 150 players. Speak to your state leaders about organising this before the window opens.

Common Mistakes

What Most Players Get Wrong

Spending resources in the days before prep phase opens. Speed-ups, pet materials and Essence Stones used casually during the week before SvS arrive at the event depleted. Hold everything from Sunday onwards.

Using resources on the wrong day. Research speed-ups used on Day 1 earn zero prep points. Know the daily scoring schedule before the event opens and ask your R4/R5 to confirm the cycle for your specific SvS.

Starting beast rallies on Day 2. The 48-hour beast event cycle means starting on Day 2 loses you one full reward payout compared to starting on Day 3. Always start on Day 3 and collect both payouts together.

Spending pet refinement materials outside the prep window. Wild Marks spent casually provide the same stat benefit but earn no event points. Save them for Day 5.

Not waiting for President skills. The President role provides a significant buff to construction, research and troop training speed. Spending speed-ups before President skills are active is one of the most common sources of missed scoring.
