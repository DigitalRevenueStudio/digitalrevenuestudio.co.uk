⚔ State vs State — Pre-Match
Matchmaking
Power Reduction
Community tactics for the Saturday–Sunday UTC matchmaking window
⚠
Community consensus — not officially confirmed. The developers state that power reduction does not influence matchmaking. However, many experienced players report consistently favourable matches after coordinated power drops. The community broadly agrees it helps. The risk of trying is zero — worst case is your normal match. Apply your own judgement.
📅 Window Saturday and Sunday UTC Actions outside this window have no effect
01
Remove Hero Gear
Unequip all hero gear from top heroes before the window opens. Hero gear is the largest single contributor to individual power — removing it creates the biggest single drop.
High
02
Reset Hero Levels via Drill Camp
Use the Drill Camp reset to lower hero levels during the window. Reduces hero power contribution to matchmaking calculations. Once per hero per day — time it carefully.
High
03
Fill Infirmaries
Pack infirmaries with troops before the window. Injured troops still count toward paper power but cannot be deployed — reducing effective fighting strength in the match evaluation.
Medium
04
Clear Inactive Player Troops
Identify players inactive for 20 or more days. Their power still contributes to matchmaking. State leadership burning inactive troops before the window removes dead weight from the state's evaluated power.
Medium
05
Coordinate Top 100–150 Players Simultaneously
The algorithm weighs top players most heavily. Scattered individual actions have little effect. Simultaneous gear removal and hero resets across the top 100 to 150 produces the largest collective power drop. R4/R5 must organise this.
Highest
💬 How to Coordinate
1.R4/R5 agrees an exact time in state Discord or alliance chat
2.All top 100 to 150 players act simultaneously — ideally within the same hour
3.Remove gear and reset heroes at the same time for maximum combined drop
4.Re-equip gear after the window closes — Sunday UTC end
