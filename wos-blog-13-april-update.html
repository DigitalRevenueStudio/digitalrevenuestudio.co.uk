Flame Tech, T12 and What Matters for F2P

The April 15 update introduced Flame Tech, Exalted Troops (T12) and the Icefire Warhymn League. Here is what changed and what F2P players should actually care about.

Digit • Digital Revenue Studio April 2026

The April 15 update was one of the larger content drops WOS has had this year. Three things stand out: Flame Tech and T12 troops, the Icefire Warhymn League, and a small quality-of-life change to the Lighthouse that is directly relevant to Flame and Fangs players.

Flame Tech and Exalted Troops (T12)

Exalted Troops are the new T12 tier, unlocked through the War Academy once your state reaches the required age and you have already unlocked Helios Troops (T11). The Flame Tech research branch opens up alongside this and further improves stats and deployment capacity for each troop type.

The materials required are Steel, Refined Fire Crystals and Fire Crystal Shards. The exact quantities are shown in-game. Note that you cannot unlock Exalted Marksman unless you have already unlocked Helios Marksman — each troop type must be unlocked in order.

For F2P players: T12 is a late-game milestone that requires significant Refined Fire Crystal investment. Focus on maximising your Refined FC acquisition through Flame and Fangs and regular event spending before targeting T12 directly. The power gap between T11 and T12 is real but not so large that rushing it at the expense of other progression makes sense.

Icefire Warhymn League: A New Solo Competition

The Icefire Warhymn League is a new solo competitive event with four phases: Sign-Up, Qualifier, Elimination and Pinnacle. No troops are lost during the event, which makes it accessible regardless of your troop depth. Top players can earn permanent Daybreak Island decorations and the overall champion receives a unique title.

A match prediction system is included where you can wager on outcomes for additional rewards. The format is similar to the Tundra Ammunition League if you have played that before.

The Lighthouse Change You Should Know About

A small but useful update: a 24-hour countdown reminder now appears on the Intel screen before Flame and Fangs begins. This makes it easier to remember to complete but not claim your Sunday 08:00 missions for the bonus cores trick. If you use our Flame and Fangs guide, this reminder now does some of the work for you.

Other Changes Worth Noting

Wild Brawl is now a recurring event once your state reaches the required age, with 6 attempts per session. Mercenary Prestige stages have been reduced from 50 to 25 across all difficulty levels from the next season. The Fortress Sign-Up interface now has a Cancel button for R4+ members. Hero Recruitment now lets you select the exact number of recruits rather than using a fixed multi-recruit button.

None of these are game-changing individually but the Wild Brawl change in particular means more consistent access to those rewards for states that qualify.
