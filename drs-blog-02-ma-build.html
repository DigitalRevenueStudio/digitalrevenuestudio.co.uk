How We Built Mavericks Adventures
from a Dinner Table Idea

From concept to published book series on Amazon. Logo, website, KDP publishing, blog content and a character-led brand built around a real cockapoo — all documented honestly.

6 min read Digital Revenue Studio March 2026

It started as a dinner table conversation that had been going in circles for about a year. Our cockapoo Maverick kept doing things that made us think: that would make a great story. But like most ideas, it stayed exactly where it was — at the dinner table.

At Digital Revenue Studio, one of the things we test is whether ideas actually work when you commit to them. So we committed. Here is the full story of how Mavericks Adventures went from a half-formed dinner table idea to a published book series on Amazon — and what we built around it.

The brief we set ourselves

Most KDP publishing advice focuses on the platform mechanics — how to upload, how to price, how to get reviews. We wanted to test a different hypothesis: what happens when you build the character first and treat the book as the entry point to something bigger?

The book was never just a book. It was the first product in a character-led brand. Maverick is the IP. The books, the activity packs, the social media — everything flows from him.

“Treat the character as the primary asset. The book is just the first thing you can buy.”

Building the brand before the book

Before a single page was designed, we built the brand infrastructure. Logo, colour palette, website, social identity. The MA brand needed to feel warm, adventurous and consistent whether a reader found it on Amazon, Instagram or the website.

The website launched on GitHub Pages at mavericksadventures.co.uk — connected via IONOS DNS. Free hosting, custom domain, indexed on Google from day one. GA4 and Search Console active before launch.

A separate Sender email account was set up for MA — distinct from the DRS newsletter. Parents who sign up for MA updates want Maverick content, not studio build diaries.

Publishing on Amazon KDP

Where Is Maverick? was published on Amazon KDP UK on 28 January 2026 in both paperback and Kindle format. No upfront printing costs. Available across UK, US and international Amazon marketplaces from day one. That global reach with zero print investment is the core KDP proposition for a new brand.

The royalty per paperback is modest at around £2.80. KDP works at volume, or it works as part of a series where a reader buys multiple titles. That is the MA model — build the series, build the audience, let the revenue compound over time.

Content and traffic

Seven blog posts went live on the MA website in the first two months. Each is SEO-targeted around search terms parents and gift-buyers actually use. Pinterest is active and driving evergreen traffic — pins from blog posts continue to generate visits months after publishing.

An activity pack was built and hosted directly on GitHub — free download, no email required. It serves as a free entry point to the brand and a conversion tool for the books.

What we learned

Building the brand infrastructure before the book launched was the right call. By the time the book was available, there was already a website to drive readers to, a social identity to build recognition around and an email list starting to grow.

The character model works because it is not limited to one book. Every future Maverick title adds to the same brand rather than starting from scratch. That compounding is slow at first but it builds in a way that a standalone title cannot.

The honest numbers at this stage are modest. But this is a 12-24 month build, not a 90-day result. The foundations are in place.
