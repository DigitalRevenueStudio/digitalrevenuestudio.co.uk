What Is Sender and Who Is It For?

Sender is an email marketing and automation platform. You use it to build an email list, send newsletters and set up automated sequences that go out when someone signs up, makes a purchase or hits another trigger you define.

It sits in the same category as Mailchimp, ConvertKit and Brevo — but with a significantly more generous free tier than most of its competitors. On the free plan you can have up to 2,500 subscribers and send 15,000 emails per month. For anyone building from zero that is more than enough to get started, grow a real audience and test whether email marketing actually works for your niche before spending a penny.

It is built for small businesses, content creators and anyone running an online project who wants to build a direct relationship with their audience without being dependent on social media algorithms.

How I Use It Across Three Brands

Digital Revenue Studio

The DRS email list captures people who download the free guide from the landing page. They go into a four-email welcome sequence over nine days — introducing the project, sharing the experiment overview and pointing them toward the experiments page. After the sequence ends they receive update emails when significant numbers change across any of the eight experiments.

Mavericks Adventures

The MA list captures parents and dog owners who sign up for the free Explorer Pack — a set of colouring pages and activity sheets featuring Maverick. The welcome sequence runs across five emails, introduces the character, links to the book on Amazon and sets the expectation for what future emails will look like. Open rates in the early weeks have been encouraging.

Whiteout Survival Guide

The WOS list is the most targeted of the three. Players sign up specifically for the free starter guide and the sequence delivers game strategy content across several emails before introducing the paid nine-chapter guide. The audience is highly specific and highly engaged — which is exactly the kind of list email marketing works best for.

How Easy Is It to Set Up?

Genuinely easy. I had a form live and a basic automation sequence running within an afternoon on my first attempt — and I had not used the platform before. The interface is clean, the logic for building automation flows is visual and intuitive, and the form builder produces an embed that works on any page.

Form Builder

Drag and drop. No coding needed. Produces a link to a hosted form page or an embed code within minutes.

Automation Builder

Visual flow builder. Set triggers, delays and conditions without touching code. Worked first time.

Email Editor

Block-based editor with clean templates. Easy to match to your brand colours and style.

Subscriber Management

Clean list view, tagging and segmentation even on the free plan. Simple to manage multiple brands.

The one thing to note is that the form embed outputs JSON rather than a standard HTML embed — so if you are pasting it into Google Sites or a similar platform you will need to link to the hosted form page rather than embedding it inline. This is a minor friction point but not a blocker. The hosted form page looks clean and works perfectly.

What the Free Tier Actually Covers

This is where Sender genuinely stands out from competitors. The free plan includes:

2,500 subscribers and 15,000 emails per month. For context, at the current DRS growth rate that covers the next several months of list building without any upgrade needed. Mailchimp's free tier caps at 500 subscribers. ConvertKit's free plan has no automation. Sender's free tier has both, at a scale that is actually useful.

Automation sequences, subscriber tagging, basic segmentation, a form builder and an email editor are all included on the free plan. The things you lose by not paying are advanced analytics, some premium templates and the Sender branding in the email footer — none of which matter at the early stage of building a list.

Early Results

It is too early to report meaningful open rate or click-through data — the lists are still young and the sample sizes are small enough that the numbers would not be representative. What I can say is that deliverability has been good across all three brands — emails are landing in inboxes rather than spam folders, which is the most important early signal.

The automation sequences are running cleanly. No failed sends, no technical issues and no emails disappearing into a queue. For a free platform that is a solid foundation.
