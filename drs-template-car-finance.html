Car Finance DCA Complaint Letter

Based on the letter used by Digital Revenue Studio in July 2024 — updated to reflect the 2025 court rulings. Free to use, no sign-up required.

Important note on this template

When I originally wrote to my lenders in July 2024, my letter only asked about discretionary commission arrangements (DCAs). At that point, the wider commission types had not yet been tested in court.

Following the Court of Appeal ruling in October 2024 and subsequent FCA announcements, the scope of the review expanded. This template has been updated to reflect those developments and now covers all three commission types relevant to the FCA review.

Already wrote in 2024? If you wrote asking only about DCAs and received written confirmation, your complaint is still valid. You do not need to write again.

How to use this template

  1. Check your agreement falls before 28 January 2021 — agreements after this date are outside the FCA review window.
  2. Find your lender — check old finance documents or your credit file if you are unsure.
  3. Replace every highlighted placeholder with your own details.
  4. Send directly to the lender by email — keep a copy with timestamps.
  5. You do not need a claims company — doing this yourself means you keep 100% of any redress.
  6. Note the date sent and keep any acknowledgement you receive.

Important — not all DCAs result in compensation

This is something most guides do not make clear enough. Having a DCA confirmed on your agreement does not automatically mean you are entitled to a refund. The type of DCA matters significantly.

There are three types:

Negative DCA — the broker or dealer used the arrangement to increase your interest rate in order to earn a higher commission. This is the type most likely to result in redress. This is what all three of my lenders confirmed in my case.

Neutral DCA — a DCA was present on the agreement but was not used to change your rate. Your interest rate was not affected. People in this situation are unlikely to receive compensation as they were not financially disadvantaged.

Positive DCA — the arrangement was used to lower your interest rate. While this may have reduced the broker's commission, you benefited from a better rate. No compensation is expected in this scenario.

The letter below asks your lender to confirm whether any commission arrangement was present. Once confirmed, the nature of that arrangement — and whether it disadvantaged you — will determine whether you are eligible for redress under the FCA scheme. No official figures for compensation amounts have been confirmed. The FCA redress scheme has not been finalised and until it is, no one can say with certainty what individual payouts will look like.

Full name:[Your full name]
Date of birth:[DD/MM/YYYY]
Current address:[As above]
Address at time of agreement:[If different from above]
Finance agreement number:[Check original paperwork or credit file]
Vehicle registration:[Number plate]
Approximate date of agreement:[Month and Year]
Dealership or broker used:[Name if known]

I am writing to ask a straightforward question: was any form of commission arrangement attached to my agreement?

Specifically, I would like to know whether my agreement involved any of the following:

A discretionary commission arrangement (DCA) — where the broker or dealer had the ability to set or adjust the interest rate on my agreement and received a higher commission the higher the rate they set.

A high commission arrangement — where the commission paid was equal to or greater than 35% of the total cost of credit and 10% of the loan amount.

A contractual tie — where an arrangement between you and the broker provided exclusivity or a right of first refusal.

If I was not made aware of any such arrangement at the time, I do not believe it was adequately disclosed to me. This is likely to have been unfair and may have prevented me from negotiating the terms of my agreement or seeking a better deal elsewhere.

If any of the above arrangements were present in connection with my agreement, please treat this letter as a formal complaint. I would ask that you respond within 8 weeks in line with FCA guidelines.

Please confirm receipt of this letter and confirm whether you are treating it as a complaint.

Yours sincerely,

[Your Full Name]
[Your Signature — if sending by post]

What to expect after sending

The FCA has paused the requirement for lenders to respond to complaints while a redress scheme is being finalised. If you receive a holding response this is completely normal — it does not mean your complaint has been rejected.

If the lender confirms a commission arrangement was attached, ask them to specify the type. A negative DCA — where your rate was increased for higher commission — is what the FCA review is primarily concerned with. Keep any written confirmation safe as it is important evidence.

No official compensation figures have been confirmed. Any figures you may have seen in the media are speculation based on early estimates. The FCA consultation is expected to conclude around end of May 2026, after which the redress scheme will be clarified.

For further background, MoneySavingExpert.com is a comprehensive resource. Martin Lewis has covered this topic in detail.
