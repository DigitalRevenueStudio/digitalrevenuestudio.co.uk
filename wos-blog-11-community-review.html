Why We Added Community Review
to Every Guide

Anyone can publish a guide. Not everyone gets it checked by players who actually compete at a high level. Here is why we built community review into the wiki from the ground up.

Digit • Digital Revenue Studio April 2026

Most game guides are written by one person, published once, and never checked by anyone who actually plays at a high level. Sometimes they are accurate. Sometimes they repeat outdated information that was true six months ago. Often there is no way to tell which is which.

We wanted to do something different with the WOS Wiki.

What Community Review Actually Means

Every guide on the WOS Wiki that covers competitive content: SvS prep, Bear Hunt, Foundry Battle, the server timeline — goes through a community review process before it is marked as reviewed. That means a real player, named and credited, has read it, checked the tactics against their actual experience, and either confirmed it or flagged corrections.

Reviewers are credited at the bottom of every guide with their name, alliance tag and state number. This is not just a credibility signal. It means that if something changes or a reviewer spots an error later, we know who to contact and the information can be updated quickly.

Guides under community review are marked clearly in the wiki index. They are live and readable but flagged as not yet independently verified. Guides marked as community reviewed have been checked by at least one active competitive player.

Why We Did Not Just Publish and Move On

The honest answer is that no single person has full knowledge of every aspect of WOS at a competitive level. Alliance Championship, Foundry Battle, high-level SvS — these events play out differently depending on your state age, your alliance strength and your position in the meta. A guide written purely from one perspective is going to have blind spots.

Community review does not fix everything but it catches the obvious errors and it forces us to be honest about what we know versus what we think we know.

How to Get Involved

If you play WOS at a competitive level and you spot something wrong in a guide, or you think a guide would benefit from your experience, the community hub is the place to start. Corrections are credited and made fast. If you want to formally review a guide, reach out through the community page.
