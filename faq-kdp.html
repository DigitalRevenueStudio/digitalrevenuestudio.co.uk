KDP — Common Questions
Answered Honestly

Royalties, costs, timelines and what Kindle Direct Publishing actually pays UK authors in 2026. Based on our own experience publishing on the platform.

The Basics

What is KDP?

Kindle Direct Publishing (KDP) is Amazon's self-publishing platform. It allows anyone to publish ebooks and paperbacks and sell them across Amazon's global marketplaces — including Amazon UK, US, and all other international stores. It is completely free to publish, and Amazon takes its cut only when a sale is made.

Do I need to pay anything to publish on KDP?

No. There is no upfront cost to publish. You upload your files, set your price, choose your distribution and go live. Amazon deducts royalties and printing costs from each sale rather than charging you separately.

What formats can I publish on KDP?

KDP supports two main formats: Kindle ebooks and paperback print-on-demand. There is also a hardcover option through their expanded print programme. You can publish one or both formats for the same title — they each have separate royalty structures.

Why are children's book royalties lower than text-only books?

Children's books with full-colour interior pages have significantly higher printing costs than text-only paperbacks. That printing cost is deducted before your 60% royalty is calculated, which reduces the net return per sale. The more colour pages, the higher the printing cost and the lower your net royalty at any given price point.

When does KDP pay out?

KDP pays royalties approximately 60 days after the end of the month in which the sale occurs. So a sale in January is paid in late March. Payments are made by BACS bank transfer for UK authors once you reach the minimum threshold (typically £10 for bank transfer).

Can I see sales data in real time?

Yes. KDP provides a real-time sales dashboard that shows units sold, royalties earned and orders. The data updates continuously, though royalties are marked as estimated until the payment period closes.

Discovery and What Actually Drives Sales

Will Amazon automatically promote my book?

Not in the early stages. Amazon's algorithm rewards books that are already selling — it surfaces titles with reviews, consistent sales velocity and strong keyword matching. A new book with no reviews and no initial sales traction gets minimal organic visibility. You need to drive initial traffic through your own channels, which creates the social proof the algorithm then amplifies.

How important are keywords on KDP?

Extremely. Amazon is a search engine. The seven keyword fields and two category selections you choose during the publishing setup directly determine whether your book appears in relevant searches. Choosing broad, generic keywords results in low visibility in a competitive space. Researching specific, lower-competition phrases that your target reader is actually searching is one of the highest-leverage activities in KDP publishing.

Do reviews matter?

Yes, significantly. Reviews are one of the primary signals Amazon uses to assess whether a book is worth surfacing to more readers. Even three or four genuine reviews can meaningfully improve a book's ranking compared to zero. Getting early reviews from genuine readers — friends, family, early buyers — is a legitimate and important part of launching a KDP title.

Other Common Questions

Can I publish under a pen name?

Yes. KDP allows you to set any author name for your published titles. Your legal name and payment details are associated with your KDP account but the name shown on the book and Amazon listing can be whatever you choose.

What is KDP Select and should I use it?

KDP Select is an optional programme that gives Amazon exclusive rights to your ebook for 90-day periods in exchange for inclusion in Kindle Unlimited (a subscription reading service) and access to promotional tools like Kindle Countdown Deals. The trade-off is exclusivity — you cannot sell the ebook elsewhere during the enrolment period. For authors building a wider distribution strategy, the exclusivity requirement can be limiting. For those focused entirely on Amazon, the Kindle Unlimited page-read royalties can supplement direct sale royalties usefully.

Can I update my book after publishing?

Yes. You can upload revised manuscript files, cover artwork or update your book description, keywords and categories at any time through the KDP dashboard. Updates typically go live within 24–72 hours after review.

Our Take

KDP is a legitimate low-cost publishing route. The platform works, the royalties are paid reliably and global distribution is real. The challenge is not the platform — it is discoverability. Without an audience or a keyword strategy that surfaces your book to genuinely interested buyers, sales do not happen by default. That is the honest constraint, and it applies regardless of how good the book is.
