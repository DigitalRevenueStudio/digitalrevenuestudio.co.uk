Alliance Championship —
Complete F2P Guide

Alliance Championship (AC) is a weekly alliance event where 6 alliances compete across 5 rounds, fighting in 3 lanes to win each round. The alliance that wins the most rounds finishes highest. Better results mean better tier rewards and stronger ongoing buffs for all members. This guide covers every phase, lane strategy, buff timing and community tips for getting the most out of AC as F2P.

Overview

What Is Alliance Championship?

Alliance Championship is a weekly event running Monday to Monday (UTC). Six alliances are matched against each other and compete across 5 rounds. Each round, your alliance fights one of the other five alliances in a lane-based battle. Your alliance is split into three lanes (left, centre, right) and so is theirs. Win more lanes than your opponent to win the round. Win more rounds to finish higher in the standings and advance to higher tiers.

Tiers run from Iron through Bronze, Silver, Gold, Diamond and Ultimate. Better tiers mean significantly better weekly rewards: hero shards, speed-ups and other high-value items. For competitive alliances, staying in Diamond or Ultimate is a major long-term progression goal.

Not Fortress Battle: Alliance Championship and Fortress Battle are completely different events. Fortress Battle is a separate weekly event for individual alliances capturing structures on the state map. AC is a 5-round seasonal competition between 6 matched alliances. Different mechanics, different rewards, different strategy entirely.

The 5 Phases

How the Event Works

Phase 1 — Sign-up (Mon 00:00 UTC to Wed 23:00 UTC)

Members register their march into one of three lanes. The march score (based on your troops, heroes and active buffs at the time of registration) determines fight order within the lane. Highest score fights last. You need a minimum of 10 members registered for your alliance to participate.

Critical: Register in the middle lane only. This is explained in the lane strategy section below. Do not register left or right at this stage regardless of what you think your role will be.

Phase 2 — Matchmaking (Wed 23:00 to Thu 00:00 UTC)

The game automatically matches your alliance with five others of similar tier. You cannot influence this. R4/R5 have no actions to take in this phase. Just wait for the matchup to be revealed.

Phase 3 — Preparation (5 × 11-hour windows before each round)

Before each battle round, R4/R5 can view the previous round's opponent lane layout (except Round 1, which is always hidden) and redistribute players across the three lanes to optimise for the upcoming fight. This is where the strategic lane management happens. See the lane strategy section below.

Phase 4 — Battle (5 × 1-hour rounds, ending Saturday 12:00 UTC)

Lanes fight automatically. Only the top 20 highest-scoring marches in each lane have a chance to fight. Each march can fight a maximum of twice per round. Winning individual fights earns KO rewards (resources and speed-ups) on top of the round result rewards.

Phase 5 — Completion (Sat 12:00 to Mon 00:00 UTC)

Final rankings are calculated and rewards distributed based on your alliance's tier and finishing position. Check results here and plan your next week's registration around what you learned from the matchup.

Lane Strategy

The Two Strong Lanes Approach

The most effective approach used by competitive alliances, including those that have climbed from Gold tier to Diamond and above, is to concentrate power in two lanes and sacrifice the third. Trying to spread evenly across all three lanes typically results in losing all three to a focused opponent.

Why Everyone Registers in the Middle Lane First

At sign-up, everyone registers in the centre lane only. Left and right lanes stay empty. This is deliberate. During matchmaking, your opponent cannot see your lane distribution, so they register their own members however they choose. Once matchmaking is complete and before Round 1 begins, an R4 or R5 moves players from the centre into a two-strong, one-weak configuration. Your opponent has already locked in. You have seen nothing about them yet for Round 1, but from Round 2 onwards you can see their previous round's lane layout and adjust accordingly.

Round 1 is always blind. You cannot see your opponent's lane layout before the first fight, and neither can they see yours. From Round 2 onwards, you can view their previous round's layout during the Preparation Phase, though they can change it and you will not see the update until after the round is fought.

Adjusting After Round 1

From Round 2, your R4/R5 can check the opponent's previous round configuration and attempt to place your two strong lanes where their weak lane was. This is educated guesswork, as they can see your previous layout too and will be making the same adjustment. The best alliances check battle history across multiple rounds to spot patterns, but ultimately predicting last-minute changes is not reliably possible. Focus on consistency in your own setup rather than outsmarting their adjustments.

Community tip: A common approach is to run two strong lanes and one weak lane throughout the season. Check battle history to see how your opponents have shifted their lanes across rounds, but accept that predicting their last-minute changes is guesswork. One alliance noted their opponent ran weak-left for three rounds in a row, then changed it at the last moment. You cannot always predict that.

Buff Timing

Locking In Your Buffs

This is one of the most impactful things an individual player can do in Alliance Championship. All PvP buffs (Minister of Strategy, pet skills, battle buffs) are locked in at the moment you register your march. If you register without buffs active, your march score and combat performance will be significantly lower for the entire event.

How to Register Correctly

Wait until after your Bear Trap session when your pet buffs are already active. Register your march during that window so your pet skills are locked in. If your alliance coordinates buff timing around Bear Trap, align your registration with that. The goal is to have Minister of Strategy and pet buffs simultaneously active when you hit register.

Never re-register without buffs active. If you change formation or re-register your march after buffs have expired, all previously locked buffs are wiped. Your march will be weaker for the entire event. If you need to change anything, make sure all buffs are active again before doing so.

Troop Setup

What to Deploy

Use your three strongest heroes, specifically those that carry the most troops and escorts. Hero quality matters more than hero level in AC since the top 20 scores per lane fight, so maximising march size and escort capacity is the priority.

For troop composition, the community consensus across multiple alliances and servers points to a few common ratios:

Community Troop Ratio Suggestions
50 / 20 / 30 Infantry / Lancer / Marksman — widely used, good general balance
45 / 25 / 30 Infantry / Lancer / Marksman — slight lancer increase, also commonly used

These are community suggestions from active players across multiple servers and alliances. The optimal ratio may vary depending on your hero lineup and server meta — treat these as a strong starting point rather than fixed rules.

Rally capacity matters: Add Rally Capacity boosts before dispatching and ensure pet skills that increase march size are active. Getting more troops into your march can make a meaningful difference to your score and combat performance.

Common Mistakes

What Loses Alliance Championship

Registering without buffs active — the most costly individual mistake. Buffs lock at registration. Registering without them means fighting the entire event at reduced power.

Spreading evenly across all three lanes — results in three average lanes rather than two strong ones. A focused opponent will beat all three. Two strong lanes and one sacrificed lane wins more rounds.

Registering in left or right lane at sign-up — limits R4/R5's ability to redistribute players before Round 1. Everyone in centre gives leadership full flexibility.

Re-registering after buffs expire — wipes all locked buffs. If you need to change your march, reactivate all buffs first.
