Bear Hunt:
The Complete F2P Guide

Bear Hunt is one of the most resource-efficient events in the game and one of the most commonly underestimated. This guide covers how it works, how starting vs joining a rally changes everything, which heroes to use at your generation level and the rules that separate average scores from great ones.

What Bear Hunt Is

The Event Explained

⏰
When It Runs

Bear Hunt runs as a regular alliance event approximately every 47 hours. Your alliance coordinates to attack increasingly powerful bears. The stronger the bear, the better the rewards. Timing and coordination significantly affect the total reward pool.

💰
Why It Matters

Bear Hunt is the primary source of Essence Stones and Hero Gear Enhancement XP in the game. These are essential for Chief Gear progression. Missing sessions consistently creates a gear gap that is difficult to recover from.

🤝
Alliance Coordination

The event is designed around coordination. A well-organised alliance targeting the right bears at the right time dramatically outperforms a disorganised one with higher individual power. Strategy matters more than raw stats.

↔
Two Different Roles

Starting a rally and joining one are completely different roles with different hero requirements. Using the wrong heroes for the wrong role actively hurts your alliance's score. Understanding the distinction is the most important mechanic in this guide.

Starting vs Joining

Two Roles, Two Different Setups

This is the mechanic most beginners miss. The hero rules for starting a rally and joining one are fundamentally different.

🎯 Starting a Rally
All expedition skills from all your heroes are used to buff the rally damage ceiling
Use your best full formation — not Gina or blue quality heroes
Aim for 4-star gold heroes with Level 5 expedition skills for triple buffs
Your formation quality directly sets the damage ceiling for the whole rally
Jeronimo + Mia + Alonso is the most common strong formation across multiple generations
👥 Joining a Rally
Only your first slot hero's first expedition skill is used to buff the rally
The rest of your formation matters far less when joining
Only four member buffs can be applied to a rally total
A hero with a Level 5 expedition skill holds their buff slot and cannot be displaced
Mia is one of the best join heroes due to her double AoE damage skills

The displacement rule: If you join a rally and a stronger hero joins after you, your hero may be displaced from a buff slot based on the rally member list order. Build your join heroes to Level 5 expedition skills to hold your buff slot permanently.

Expedition Skill Tier List

Bear Trap DMG Hero Rankings

Not all heroes contribute equally to Bear Hunt damage. Your hero's expedition skills and their level determine how much damage your rally adds. Here is how the Bear Trap damage tier breaks down:

S
S Tier — Best Expedition Heroes

Evelin, Jeronimo, Jasser, Aaron, Balaguun, Rahul. Highest-value expedition skills for Bear Hunt damage. If you have these developed, prioritise them in your formation. Target 4-star gold quality with Level 5 expedition skills.

A
A Tier — Strong Alternatives

Mia, Layton, Norah, Greg, Philip. Solid expedition contributors. Mia is particularly strong for joining rallies due to her AoE damage skills alongside expedition utility. A-tier heroes are the realistic target for most F2P players.

B
B Tier — Usable

Wayne, Renee, Flint (lower star). Fine to use if you do not have better options at your current development stage. Do not over-invest in these heroes specifically for Bear Hunt.

C
C Tier — Last Resort

If you do not have any of the above ready: join with a low-star hero in slot one, or join with no heroes filling buff slots. An empty buff slot is better than the wrong hero occupying it.

Key Rules

What Separates Good Scores From Great Ones

  • Buff slots fill top-to-bottom from the rally member list. The first four rally members from the top of the list take the buff slots. Joining early gives you a better chance of holding a buff position.
  • A hero with a Level 5 expedition skill cannot be displaced. Once your hero holds a buff slot with a maxed expedition skill, they keep that position regardless of who joins after. Getting join heroes to Level 5 expedition skill is a priority worth front-loading.
  • Hero level and star count do not matter for joining. Only the first slot hero's first expedition skill level matters when joining a rally. A lower-level hero with a Level 5 expedition skill outperforms a high-level hero with Level 4.
  • From Gen 8+, use current generation bears. Damage bonuses from current-gen heroes scale with current-gen bears. Using older heroes on higher-tier bears reduces output. Keep your formation current as you progress.
  • Cyrille's Expert skills directly increase your Bear Hunt rewards. Developing Cyrille's Skills 2 and 3 increases the rewards you pull from every session. If you have the Expert system unlocked, this is one of the highest-value Bear Hunt investments available.

The single most important Bear Hunt habit: Show up consistently. The compounding value of consistent participation over 30 days is one of the clearest differences between fast-developing and slow-developing players at every level. If your alliance has a set time for kills, be there.

Common Mistakes

What Most Players Get Wrong

Treating Bear Hunt as optional. It is not. Bear Hunt is the primary source of Chief Gear materials in the game. Missing sessions consistently creates a gear progression gap that compounds over weeks and months into a significant power disadvantage.

Using the wrong heroes for your role. Starting a rally and joining one require different setups. Using your join formation when you are the rally leader, or using your start formation heroes when joining, actively hurts your alliance's score. Know which role you are playing before every session.

Not developing Cyrille's Expert skills. Cyrille's Skills 2 and 3 provide direct Bear Hunt damage and reward bonuses. Players who unlock the Expert system but ignore Cyrille miss a significant source of compounding Bear Hunt value.

Not coordinating target selection with your alliance. Attacking a bear your alliance cannot reliably kill wastes stamina with no reward. Attacking too weak a bear caps your reward tier below what your power level could achieve. Ask your R4/R5 which bears to target each session.

Not checking Hospital capacity before major hunts. When your Hospital is full and your troops take damage, the overflow troops die permanently rather than becoming wounded and recoverable. Before any large Bear Hunt rally, check that your Hospital has enough free space to absorb potential casualties. Heal wounded troops beforehand to free up capacity if needed.
