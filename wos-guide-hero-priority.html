Heroes are the backbone of your account. Invest in the wrong ones and you waste months of shards, XP and skill manuals. This guide covers which heroes to build as F2P, in what order, and how generations change your priorities over time.

WOS Wiki Hero Priority F2P Strategy March 2026
The Core Rule

Focus on 2–3 Heroes Only

The most common hero mistake in WOS is upgrading every hero a little. This feels productive but it is the slowest path to strength. Splitting shards, XP and skill manuals across your whole roster leaves you with ten mediocre heroes instead of three strong ones.

The rule: Pick 2–3 heroes and put everything into them. Aim to get your core heroes to Star Level 4 — this unlocks all their skills and gives you the full benefit of their kit. A fully built Molly outperforms four partially built heroes in every game mode.

New generations of heroes arrive roughly every 80 days. This does not mean your current heroes become useless — it means you need to be smart about which ones you invest heavily in, because some heroes remain viable for many generations while others get outclassed quickly.

Generation 1 — F2P Priorities

Who to Build First

Hero Gen Why Build F2P Priority
Molly Gen 1 Versatile lancer, strong in arena and events. Easy to star up. Pays back every resource invested. ★ Build First
Natalia Gen 1 Strong infantry attacker with a good skill kit. Note: Natalia is only available from VIP packs — she is not accessible to true F2P players. If you have her, she is worth building. If not, do not worry — there are better F2P paths. Spender/VIP Only
Sergey Gen 1 Reliable infantry defender. Excellent for Crazy Joe defence and early Exploration. Easy to obtain. ★ Build Early
Bahiti Gen 1 Good early tank, decent crowd control. Useful in Exploration. Lower long-term ceiling than the top three. Good Secondary
Jeronimo Gen 1 Elite infantry rally lead — the best Gen 1 hero overall. Won via Hall of Chiefs top 10. Important: General (Gold Mythic) Shards cannot be used on Jeronimo — only Jeronimo-specific shards from HoC rewards work. Do not waste general shards trying to star him up. HoC Target
Zinman Gen 1 Lucky Wheel hero. Strong but expensive. Do not spend Gold Mythic Shards on him — save them for Gen 2. Skip (Gold Shards)

Gold Mythic Shard rule: Do not spend your Gold (General Mythic) Hero Shards on Gen 1 heroes like Zinman. Save them for Gen 2 — but do not use them on Flint. Flint is a Lucky Wheel hero and should be maxed entirely through wheel spins during Hall of Chiefs. Your Gold Shards belong on Alonso — aim for 4 stars. This is the correct F2P path.

Generation 2 — What Changes

When Gen 2 Unlocks

Gen 2 arrives around Day 40. Three new heroes become available: Flint, Philly and Alonso. This changes your investment priorities.

Hero Gen Why Build F2P Priority
Flint Gen 2 Strong infantry frontline tank. Won via Lucky Wheel (Hall of Chiefs Day 2). This is where your saved Gems go — spin for Flint during HoC. ★ Priority Target
Philly Gen 2 Replaces Jeronimo as the Hall of Chiefs Season Hero in Gen 2. Excellent support hero. Build alongside Flint. ★ HoC Target
Alonso Gen 2 The correct F2P Gold Shard target in Gen 2. Aim for 4 stars using Gold (General Mythic) Shards — this is the most efficient use of your saved shards. Do not spend shards on Flint (wheel only). Alonso 4 stars is the proper F2P Gen 2 path. ★ Gold Shard Target

Continue building Molly into Gen 2. She remains relevant across generations — do not abandon your Gen 1 investments. Add Flint (via wheel) and work towards Alonso 4 stars with Gold Shards. If you have Natalia and built her, she stays useful — but she is not a F2P-accessible hero so do not plan around her.

How to Upgrade

The Correct Upgrade Order

🔴
Star Level First
Use shards to ascend your core heroes. Aim for Star Level 4 — this unlocks all skills. Stars matter more than level in the early game.
📚
Skills Second — Expedition Before Exploration
Expedition skills affect combat events (Bear Hunt, Crazy Joe, SvS). Exploration skills affect the passive Exploration mode. Prioritise Expedition skills for your combat heroes. For Molly specifically: Exploration Skill 1 and Expedition Skill 2 first.
⬆
Level Up Gradually
Keep your core heroes within 1–2 levels of each other as you raise them. Do not max one hero's level at the expense of leaving others far behind — balance matters for team composition.
❌
Blue Heroes — Gathering Skills Are the Exception
Blue (Epic) combat heroes have a low star cap and limited long-term value in combat — do not invest combat Skill Manuals there. However, blue gathering heroes are different. Gathering bonus skills on blue heroes directly increase your resource output every single day. Investing Skill Manuals in blue gathering heroes' gathering bonus skills is correct F2P play — do not skip this.
Shard Rules

How to Manage Hero Shards

✓ Do

Save all Skill Manuals for your 2–3 core heroes. Spend Hero Shards on the same generation as your current active roster. Use Hall of Chiefs Day 2 Gems on Lucky Wheel for the featured hero.

✕ Don't

Do not waste Gold Mythic Shards on Gen 1 heroes if Gen 2 is close. Do not spend Gold Shards on Flint — he is a wheel hero, max him through Lucky Wheel spins only. Do not split shards across every new hero that arrives.

★ Exclusive Gear

Only invest in Exclusive Gear for heroes that will remain core across multiple generations. Safe long-term investments: Jeronimo, Alonso, Mia (Gen 3). These heroes stay relevant even as new gens arrive.

⚠ The Generation Rule

Newer gen heroes are generally stronger — but that does not mean abandoning your built heroes. A fully built Gen 1 hero beats a half-built Gen 2 hero. Invest in what you have until the new generation is actually available.
