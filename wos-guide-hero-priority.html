Hero Upgrade Priority:
F2P Guide

Heroes are the backbone of your account. Invest in the wrong ones and you waste months of shards, XP and skill manuals. This guide covers which heroes to build as F2P, in what order, and how generations change your priorities over time.

The Core Rule

Focus on 2 to 3 Heroes Only

The most common hero mistake in WOS is upgrading every hero a little. This feels productive but it is the slowest path to strength. Splitting shards, XP and skill manuals across your whole roster leaves you with ten mediocre heroes instead of three strong ones.

Pick 2 to 3 heroes and put everything into them. Aim to get your core heroes to Star Level 4: this unlocks all their skills and gives you the full benefit of their kit. A fully built Molly outperforms four partially built heroes in every game mode.

New generations of heroes arrive roughly every 80 days. This does not mean your current heroes become useless. It means you need to be strategic about which ones you invest heavily in, because some heroes remain viable for many generations while others are quickly outclassed.

How to Upgrade

The Correct Upgrade Order

1
Unlock skills before raising star level

Unlocking the next skill costs fewer shards than raising star level and often provides more immediate combat value. Work through available skills first.

2
Reach 4 stars before heavy skill manual investment

4 stars unlocks all skills for a hero. Getting your core hero to 4 stars gives you access to their full kit. This is where the majority of your shards should go.

3
Save skill manuals for core heroes only

Skill manuals are scarce. Using them on heroes outside your top 2 to 3 is almost always a mistake. Reserve them exclusively for current priority heroes.

4
Do not use skill manuals on blue heroes

Blue heroes are replaced quickly. Skill manuals spent on blue heroes are permanently wasted. Only use them on purple or gold quality heroes you intend to develop long-term.

The Generation Rule

When to Transition Generations

A hero typically remains viable for 3 to 4 generations after their release before being meaningfully outclassed. Start transitioning your investment to a new generation's best hero when they become accessible, but do not abandon a well-developed hero for a new one you cannot yet develop properly.

The F2P transition pattern: Develop your Gen 1 core heroes to 4 stars before Gen 2 unlocks. When Gen 2 opens, shift primary shard investment to Philly while maintaining your Gen 1 heroes. Never start developing a new hero before you have a clear shard acquisition path for them.

Common Mistakes

What Most Players Get Wrong

Spreading shards across too many heroes simultaneously. Shards and skill manuals are limited resources. Spreading them across 4 or 5 heroes means none of them reach the star levels where their skills become impactful. Pick 2 to 3 and go deep.

Using Gold Mythic Shards on Zinman. Zinman is a Lucky Wheel hero requiring significant gem investment to fully develop. Gold Mythic Shards spent on him are shards not spent on Philly or future priority heroes. Save all Gold Mythic Shards for Gen 2 and beyond.

Trying to use general shards on Jeronimo. Jeronimo only accepts Jeronimo-specific shards from Hall of Chiefs rewards. General Gold Mythic Shards are wasted on him. Many players burn general shards on Jeronimo expecting it to work.

Not developing Philly in Gen 2. Philly is the primary healer in the game and remains relevant across many generations. Skipping her development in Gen 2 creates a long-term weakness in march composition that is difficult to correct later.

Using skill manuals on blue heroes. Blue quality heroes are replaced quickly. Skill manuals are too scarce to spend on heroes you will abandon within one generation. Reserve them exclusively for purple and gold quality priority heroes.
