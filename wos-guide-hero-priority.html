The Core Rule

Focus on 2 to 3 Heroes Only

The most common hero mistake in WOS is upgrading every hero a little. This feels productive but it is the slowest path to strength. Splitting shards, XP and skill manuals across a whole roster leaves you with ten mediocre heroes instead of three strong ones.

Pick 2 to 3 heroes and put everything into them. Aim to get your core heroes to Star Level 4, which unlocks all their skills and gives you the full benefit of their kit. A fully built Molly outperforms four partially built heroes in every game mode.

New hero generations arrive roughly every 80 days. This does not mean current heroes become useless — it means you need to be smart about which ones you invest heavily in, since some heroes remain viable for many generations while others get outclassed quickly.

Gold Mythic Shard rule: Do not spend your Gold (General Mythic) Hero Shards on Gen 1 heroes. Save them for Gen 2, specifically for Alonso. Do not use them on Flint either. Flint is a Lucky Wheel hero and should be maxed entirely through wheel spins during Hall of Chiefs. Gold Shards belong on Alonso.

Blue hero skill manual rule: Do not use skill manuals on blue (Rare) quality heroes. Save all skill manuals for your top 2 to 3 gold heroes. Blue hero skills provide minimal impact compared to gold hero skills at the same manual cost.

Common Mistakes

What Most Players Get Wrong

Upgrading too many heroes simultaneously. Shards and skill manuals are limited. Spreading them across 4 or 5 heroes means none reach the levels where skills become impactful. Go deep on 2 to 3 heroes per generation and move on only when they are properly developed.

Spending Gold Mythic Shards on Gen 1 heroes. Gold Mythic Shards are your most valuable hero resource. Using them on Zinman or other Gen 1 heroes wastes their long-term value. Save them for Alonso in Gen 2.

Using skill manuals on secondary heroes. Skill manuals are scarce. Using them on heroes outside your top 2 to 3 is almost always a mistake. Every manual spent on a secondary hero is a manual not spent compounding your primary heroes' power.

Investing heavily in Bahiti long-term. Bahiti is a useful Gen 1 placeholder but is outclassed quickly. Invest enough to make her functional in early game but do not pour resources into a hero you will replace within your first month.

Not confirming shard acquisition routes before committing to a hero. Some heroes are accessible via Lucky Wheel, some via events and some only via direct spending. Always confirm how you will acquire shards before building a hero into your core lineup.
