Hunting Guide

Polar Terrors are the highest-stamina-efficiency target in Whiteout Survival. When you know how to hunt them correctly — the right heroes, the right rally approach, and when to prioritise them over regular beasts — they become one of the best F2P reward sources in the game.

The Basics

What Are Polar Terrors?

Polar Terrors are the large, high-power enemies on the world map that require alliance rallies to defeat. Unlike regular Beasts which can be attacked solo, Polar Terrors need multiple players to bring down — your alliance calls a rally and members join with their troops.

Beasts vs Polar Terrors

Which Is Better to Hunt?

Regular Beasts
Solo attack — 10 stamina each
1 point per beast. Can be attacked without alliance help. Good for early-game resources and progression missions. Higher-level beasts (26+) give the most SvS points per stamina of any single beast.

Best for: SvS Beast Slay day (Level 26+ beasts), early game stamina efficiency, solo play.
Polar Terrors
Rally required — 20–25 stamina
3 points per Polar Terror. More stamina per hunt but significantly better points-to-stamina ratio than standard beasts. Rewards include Hardened Alloy, resources and event points.

Best for: Beast Slay event, SvS Day 3 rally points, alliance coordination sessions.

The efficiency calculation: With Gina at max skill (20 stamina per Polar Terror), 3 Polar Terrors cost 60 stamina for 9 points. 9 regular beasts cost 72 stamina for 9 points. Polar Terrors are more stamina-efficient for point gathering — but only when your alliance is actively calling rallies to join.

Stamina Efficiency

The Gina Advantage

Gina is the single most important hero for hunting. Her Endurance Training skill reduces stamina costs, and her Quick Paced skill increases march speed — meaning faster attacks and more hunts per session.

Rally Strategy

How to Hunt as a Team

🔁
Enable auto-join rallies. This is the single most important setting for Polar Terror hunting. Auto-join means you automatically participate in alliance rallies even when you are not actively watching the screen. Go to Settings and turn it on — never miss a rally again.
💬
Coordinate with your alliance on timing. Polar Terror rallies are most efficient when multiple players are online simultaneously. Your R4/R5 should announce rally sessions — be online for them if you can.
📉
Adjust the level if rallies are failing. If your alliance is calling rallies that keep failing because not enough troops join in time, drop the Polar Terror level. A completed lower-level rally is worth more than a failed high-level one.
🤝
Ask for help if your rally stalls. If you call a rally and it looks like it might fail, post in alliance chat. Your stamina is refunded if a rally fails — but it is better to complete it. A strong alliance member joining can make the difference.
⚡
Pair with Walis Bokan for rapid attacks. Walis Bokan's Huntsman's Gift skill increases Wilderness March Speed by 100%. Stack this with Gina when leading rallies for the fastest possible attack cycle — more rallies per hour means more stamina converted to points.
Event Timing

When Polar Terrors Matter Most

Beast Slay event (runs every two weeks, two days): This is the primary window to empty your stamina on Polar Terrors. The event rewards points for every Beast and Polar Terror defeated — Polar Terrors give 3 points versus 1 point for regular beasts. Save stamina in the days leading up to the event and spend it all during the two-day window.

SvS Day 3 — Beast Slay day: During State vs State prep phase, Day 3 is the Beast Slay scoring day. Polar Terror rallies give 30,000 points per rally (25 stamina). This is the highest points-per-stamina action of the entire SvS prep phase. Save all stamina for Day 3. Lead rallies — joining someone else's rally does not score SvS points for you.

⏰
Collect stamina from the Storehouse twice daily. The Storehouse drops 120 stamina at 12:00 UTC and again at 24:00 UTC. You must collect it before the next drop or you lose it. Set a reminder if needed — this is free stamina that many players miss.
📋
Do not complete your Intel mission the day before a Beast Slay event. The daily Intel mission sometimes contains beast kills as an objective. Completing it the day before the event wastes kills that could have scored event points. Hold off until the event starts.
