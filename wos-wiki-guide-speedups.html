⏱ Resource Strategy

Speed-Ups
When to Use
When to Wait

Speed-ups are the most misused resource in Whiteout Survival. New players burn them on five-minute timers and wonder why they fall behind. This guide explains exactly when to use them, when to save them, and which events make them most valuable.

WOS Wiki Resource Strategy F2P March 2026
Types of Speed-Ups

Know What You Have

🏗
Construction Speed-Ups
Used to reduce building upgrade timers. Your most common type. Highest value used on long Furnace upgrades or during Hall of Chiefs Day 1.
🔬
Research Speed-Ups
Reduce research timers only. Save for Hall of Chiefs Day 4 or SvS research days — using them outside events wastes scoring potential.
⚔
Training Speed-Ups
Reduce troop training timers. Save for Hall of Chiefs Day 3 and SvS training days. Outside events, only use to keep queues from stalling overnight.
⚡
General Speed-Ups
Can be used for any timer. Your most flexible resource. Save these as a buffer for unexpected long timers or event days where you run short of the specific type.

The core mistake: Using speed-ups on short timers feels satisfying but is the slowest path forward. A 5-minute timer you burn a 1-hour speed-up on is 55 minutes of potential wasted. The habit that separates strong F2P players is simple — only use speed-ups when the timer is long or the event demands it.

Event Value

Where Speed-Ups Score The Most Points

The highest value use of any speed-up is during an event that scores points for using them. Spending the same speed-up during an event vs outside an event can mean the difference between hitting a milestone reward or missing it.

The Rules

Speed-Up Rules for F2P

📦
Save until an event demands them. The ideal time to spend big is Hall of Chiefs and SvS — both events score points for construction, research and training speed-ups specifically. Hold your stockpile for these windows.
⏰
The overnight rule. The one exception to saving everything: if a long Furnace upgrade (6 hours or more) would sit idle overnight while you sleep, using a speed-up to complete it is a good use. Idle queues are wasted time.
🔟
Never use speed-ups on timers under 30 minutes. The time saving is negligible. Let short timers run naturally and save every speed-up for what matters.
🎯
Match the type to the event day. Using a Research speed-up on Hall of Chiefs Day 1 (Construction day) scores nothing — the points only trigger for the correct category. Always check which type an event day rewards before spending.
📊
Aim for milestones, not rankings. In F2P, you cannot out-speed-up a whale. But milestone point thresholds are achievable with good hoarding habits. Know your milestone targets and spend enough to hit them — not more.
💰
Gems follow the same logic. Gems are effectively premium speed-ups. Save them for Lucky Wheel during Hall of Chiefs Day 2. Do not spend Gems on construction or research speed-ups — that is the fastest way to fall behind as F2P.

The compound effect: Every speed-up you save this week is still available next week. Every speed-up you burn casually is gone. The players who look like they have been playing for years at Day 90 are usually the ones who saved their resources from Day 1. Start the habit early and it pays dividends for the entire life of the account.
