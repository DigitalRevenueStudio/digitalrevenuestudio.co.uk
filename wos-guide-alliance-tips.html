🤝 Alliance Guide

Alliance Tips:
Joining, Contributing
and Progressing

Your alliance is not just a clan tag. It determines your protection, your event rewards, your resource gathering speed and how fast your city grows. Choosing the right one and contributing correctly is one of the highest-leverage decisions you make in WOS.

Why It Matters

What Your Alliance Actually Does For You

New players often treat alliances as background — a box to tick. The reality is that an active, well-organised alliance accelerates your progression in ways that cannot be replicated solo. Passive production boosts, shared events, protection from attacks and access to Alliance Technology all compound over time.

🛡
Protection
Most states operate NAP agreements (non-aggression pacts). Being in a top-5 alliance means attackers think twice. Solo players and weak-alliance members are easy targets for resource raids.
⚡
Alliance Technology
Alliance tech boosts gathering speed, construction speed and troop stats for every member. A well-developed tech tree gives significant passive advantages — you benefit just by being a member.
🎯
Events
Bear Hunt, Crazy Joe, Alliance Mobilisation, Fortress Battle — the best events in the game are alliance-only. You simply cannot access them without an active alliance.
📍
Territory and Gathering
Alliance territory provides faster and safer resource gathering nodes. Gathering inside your alliance zone reduces risk and often provides a speed bonus. Position your city within the territory when you join.
Finding the Right One

How to Choose Your Alliance

Not all alliances are equal. Joining the wrong one is almost as bad as being solo. Here is what to look for:

✅
Look at the Alliance Rankings. Top-5 alliances have the most territory, the strongest tech and the best event participation. The difference in progression between a top-5 and a bottom-10 alliance is significant and compounds over months.
✅
Check activity before committing. Look at the alliance chat before joining. Is anyone talking? Are events being organised? A silent alliance is usually an inactive one. Active chat and regular event announcements are the signs you want.
📍
Teleport to the alliance zone immediately after joining. A city isolated from its alliance is a target. Move within the territory the day you join — do not sit outside the zone.
📋
Read the alliance rules. Most organised alliances have rules on NAP agreements, rally obligations and event participation expectations. Know them before you act.
⚠
Do not stay in an inactive alliance out of loyalty. If your alliance stops running events or starts shrinking, move. Your progression suffers directly. Switch early while your options are still open.

Unlock requirement: You need Furnace Level 7 to access the Alliance tab. Join as soon as it unlocks. Every day without an alliance is a day of missed tech bonuses, missed event points and missed protection.

How to Contribute

What F2P Players Should Actually Do

You do not need to be a big spender to be a valuable alliance member. Consistent contribution in the right areas makes more difference than occasional large spending.

💰
Donate to Alliance Technology daily. Every donation contributes to tech that benefits everyone. Prioritise tech nodes that boost gathering speed and construction — these compound across all members over time.
📱
Be online for events. Crazy Joe, Bear Hunt and Fortress Battle all reward online participation heavily. Waves 7, 14 and 17 of Crazy Joe only target active players — being online during these is directly worth points for everyone.
🐻
Fill Bear Hunt rallies. Even if you cannot lead a rally, joining with all your stamina contributes directly to the alliance score. Empty your stamina on the bear every time without exception.
🚧
Help with construction. When an alliance member posts a construction request, tap the help button. It costs you nothing and saves them real time. One of the easiest ways to be a valued alliance member.
💬
Communicate. If you are going offline for an extended period, say so in alliance chat. If an important event is happening and you cannot attend, let your R4/R5 know. Silence is mistaken for inactivity and leads to being removed from good alliances.
Alliance Ranks

What the Ranks Mean

RankTitleWhat They Do
R5LeaderFull control — sets rules, manages membership, calls major events, controls territory decisions
R4OfficerCan start events (Bear Hunt, Crazy Joe), manage members and issue instructions. The people who run day-to-day operations
R3EliteTrusted members — often experienced players given more responsibility. May assist with rally coordination
R2VeteranActive members who have demonstrated consistent participation over time
R1MemberStandard starting rank for new joiners. Participate in events, donate to tech, follow instructions

As a new F2P member: You will start at R1. Focus on consistent participation, filling rallies and donating to tech. Rank promotions come from demonstrating reliability, not power. Small accounts who show up for every event are valued more than large accounts who are never online.

Alliance Events

The Events That Matter Most

🐻
Bear Hunt
Runs every ~47 hours. R4/R5 opens a rally against a bear. Send all stamina every time — rewards include Essence Stones for Chief Gear. Join the strongest rally available rather than opening your own unless you have a strong hero setup for leading.
🌊
Crazy Joe
20 waves of bandits, 40 minutes. Send all Infantry and Lancers to allies before it starts. Keep Marksmen home to defend your own city. Be online for waves 7, 14 and 17 — elite waves that target active players for additional points.
📋
Alliance Mobilisation
Multi-day event with daily tasks covering gathering, hunting and construction. Coordinate with your alliance on which tasks to focus. Completing tasks alongside other members multiplies rewards for everyone.
🏰
Fortress Battle
8-week seasonal competition, weekly on Fridays. Alliances fight for control of 16 structures on the world map. Hold a structure for 30 minutes or at the end of the 2-hour battle window to claim it. Held structures provide ongoing buffs to all alliance members. Leadership registers targets in advance.
🔧
Foundry Battle
Bi-weekly, one-hour, 30v30 alliance combat on Sundays. Requires Furnace Level 16+. Teams capture facilities to accumulate Arsenal Points. F2P players contribute most effectively in Phase 3 by gathering from Weapon Workshops rather than contesting the main objectives.
🧊
Polar Terror Rallies
Alliance members call rallies against Polar Terrors on the world map. Enable Auto-Join so you participate automatically even while offline. Among the best stamina-to-reward ratios of any event, especially during SvS Beast Slay day.
Common Mistakes

What Most Players Get Wrong

Staying in an inactive alliance too long. The cost compounds daily through missed event rewards, no passive tech boosts and no protection. If your alliance consistently misses Bear Hunt and Crazy Joe, it is time to move. Loyalty to an inactive alliance costs you real progression.

Joining the first alliance that invites you. Random invitations are often from alliances filling seats rather than building a strong roster. Take a few minutes to check activity levels, R4/R5 presence and recent event participation before accepting.

Not contributing to Alliance Technology. Alliance Tech boosts apply to every member including you. Skipping donations means everyone's progression slows, including your own. Prioritise the nodes your R4/R5 highlights in alliance announcements.

Sending Marksmen to allies during Crazy Joe. Marksmen are your highest-value defensive troops. Sending them out leaves your city exposed during an event specifically designed to attack it. Keep all Marksmen home and send Infantry and Lancers to allies.

Going offline without communicating during major events. Disappearing during SvS battle phase, Crazy Joe or Fortress Battle without notice leaves your alliance short-handed at the worst possible moment. A quick message in alliance chat is all it takes.
